CONTENT:  
Mutual Funds (MFs) are a popular investment choice that pools money from multiple investors to invest in a diversified portfolio of stocks, bonds, or other securities. Understanding key terms and investment methods is essential for making informed decisions.

Key Terms:  
- **Net Asset Value (NAV):** Calculated by dividing the total net assets of a scheme by the units issued. NAV changes daily based on the market value of the securities held.  
- **Assets Under Management (AUM):** Reflects the current value of an MF scheme's assets. A higher AUM generally indicates a larger client base and greater investor trust.  
- **Portfolio:** The collection of stocks, bonds, or other securities that an investor or fund manager invests in.  
- **Financial Year:** A one-year period used by companies and governments for financial reporting and budgeting.  
- **Load:** Commission or sales charge applied when buying or selling MF units. Types include:  
  - Front-end load: Charged when buying shares  
  - Back-end load: Charged when selling shares  
  - No load: No sales charge  
- **Diversification:** Holding a variety of investments across different sectors or asset classes to reduce risk.  
- **Redemption:** The process of selling MF units back to the fund. Redemptions are usually processed within a few days.  
- **Benchmark:** A standard index against which the performance of an MF is measured.  
- **Capital Gains:** Payments made to shareholders from profits realized on the sale of securities in the fund’s portfolio. These can be short-term or long-term and are subject to capital gains tax.  
- **Dividend Reinvestment:** An option allowing investors to use dividend payouts to purchase additional shares in the fund.  
- **Total Return:** A measure of an MF’s performance including changes in NAV, dividends, and capital gain distributions.  
- **Risk Profile:** An investor’s capacity to endure loss. Different MFs have varying risk levels suited to different investor profiles.

Investment Methods:  
- **Lump Sum Investment:** Investing a large sum of money at once. Suitable for investors expecting favorable market conditions and seeking high returns. This method carries risk due to market fluctuations.  
- **Systematic Investment Plan (SIP):** Investing small amounts regularly (monthly or quarterly). SIPs reduce the impact of market volatility and allow investors to buy more units when prices are low.

KEY POINTS:  
- NAV and AUM are critical indicators of mutual fund value and trust.  
- Loads affect the cost of buying or selling mutual fund units.  
- Diversification helps reduce investment risk.  
- Capital gains from mutual funds are taxable.  
- SIPs help mitigate market volatility compared to lump sum investments.
