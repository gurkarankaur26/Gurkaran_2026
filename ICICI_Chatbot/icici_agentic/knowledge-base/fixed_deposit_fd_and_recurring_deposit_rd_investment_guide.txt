When to Invest in FD and RD?  
- Fixed Deposits (FD) generally offer higher interest rates compared to Recurring Deposits (RD).  
- FD is suitable when you have a lump sum amount to invest for a fixed tenure.  
- RD is ideal when you do not have a lump sum but can save a fixed amount regularly every month. It helps in achieving medium-term financial goals through disciplined savings.  
- Both FD and RD are beneficial for all classes of investors, especially small investors in lower tax slabs due to lower or non-taxability of interest income and attractive interest payouts.  

Added Flexibility in RD - Flexible RD (iWish)  
- ICICI Bank offers a flexible RD product called iWish, designed to address concerns about sustaining fixed monthly instalments.  
- iWish allows customers to make deposits as per their convenience rather than fixed monthly instalments.  
- It combines the benefits of regular savings with the option to add lump sum amounts whenever available, providing greater flexibility in savings.  

KEY POINTS:  
- FD is best for lump sum investments; RD is best for regular monthly savings.  
- Both products are suitable for small investors and those in lower tax brackets.  
- Interest earned on FD and RD is subject to tax as per applicable laws.  
- Flexible RD (iWish) allows variable deposits and lump sum additions, enhancing savings convenience.  
- These products help meet medium-term financial goals with disciplined savings and attractive interest rates.
