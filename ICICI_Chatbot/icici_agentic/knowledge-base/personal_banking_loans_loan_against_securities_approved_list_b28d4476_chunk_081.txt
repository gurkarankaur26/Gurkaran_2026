TITLE:  
Motilal Oswal and Nippon India Mutual Funds - Key Schemes and Options

SOURCE: ICICI Bank  
CATEGORY: Mutual Funds  
TAGS: Mutual Funds, Liquid Funds, Banking and PSU Funds, Corporate Bond Funds, Investment Options, Dividend Options, Growth Options

CONTENT:  
This document provides an overview of select mutual fund schemes offered by Motilal Oswal and Nippon India, highlighting various investment options such as Growth, Dividend (IDCW), and Bonus plans. These funds cater to different investor preferences with options for direct and regular plans, and dividend payout frequencies including daily, weekly, monthly, quarterly, and fortnightly.

**Motilal Oswal Liquid Fund**  
- Available in Direct and Regular plans.  
- Options include Growth and Income Distribution cum Capital Withdrawal (IDCW) with payout frequencies: Daily, Weekly, Fortnightly, Monthly, and Quarterly.  
- Suitable for investors seeking liquidity and regular income.

**Nippon India Banking and PSU Fund**  
- Available in Direct and Regular plans.  
- Options include Growth, Bonus, and IDCW with payout frequencies: Weekly, Monthly, and Quarterly.  
- Focuses on investments in banking and public sector undertakings (PSU) stocks, suitable for investors seeking sector-specific exposure.

**Nippon India Corporate Bond Fund**  
- Available in Direct and Regular plans.  
- IDCW options with payout frequencies: Daily, Weekly, Monthly, and Quarterly.  
- Invests primarily in corporate bonds, suitable for investors seeking fixed income with relatively lower risk.

KEY POINTS:  
- Multiple mutual fund schemes with various dividend payout options to suit different investor needs.  
- Direct and Regular plans available for all schemes.  
- Dividend payout frequencies include Daily, Weekly, Fortnightly, Monthly, and Quarterly depending on the scheme.  
- Growth options available for capital appreciation.  
- Sector-specific funds like Banking and PSU Fund provide targeted investment opportunities.  
- Corporate Bond Fund offers fixed income investment with regular dividend options.
