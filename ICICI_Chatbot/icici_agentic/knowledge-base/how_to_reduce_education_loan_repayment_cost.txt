TITLE: How to Reduce Education Loan Repayment Cost  
SOURCE: ICICI Bank  
CATEGORY: Education Loan, Loan Repayment, Personal Finance  
TAGS: Education Loan, Loan Repayment, Interest Rates, Tax Benefits, Loan Tenure, EMI, Collateral  

CONTENT:  
Education Loans are a popular financial solution for Indian students seeking higher education, supported by low interest rates and attractive offers from banks like ICICI Bank. However, repaying these loans can be challenging. Here are effective ways to reduce the cost of education loan repayment and make the loan more affordable:

1. **Prefer Loans with Collateral**  
   - Secured education loans backed by collateral such as residential property, Fixed Deposits (FDs), or shares generally offer lower interest rates compared to unsecured loans.  
   - Collateral increases the chances of loan approval and reduces the overall cost of repayment due to lower interest rates.

2. **Pay Full EMI During the Moratorium Period**  
   - The moratorium period typically starts after course completion and lasts about 6 months, during which only the interest component is payable.  
   - Paying full EMIs (principal + interest) during this period helps reduce the principal amount early, leading to significant interest savings over the loan tenure.

3. **Opt for a Shorter Tenure**  
   - Unsecured education loans are usually available for up to 8 years, while secured loans can extend up to 10 years.  
   - Although longer tenures reduce monthly EMI amounts, they increase the total interest paid. Choosing a shorter tenure reduces overall interest cost.  
   - Use an Education Loan Calculator to assess affordable EMI amounts and the impact of tenure on repayment.

4. **Tax Benefits under Section 80(E)**  
   - Interest paid on education loans is eligible for tax deduction under Section 80(E) of the Income Tax Act.  
   - There is no upper limit on the deduction amount; the entire interest paid in a financial year can be claimed.  
   - This benefit applies to loans taken for yourself, your spouse, or your children.

KEY POINTS:  
- Secured education loans with collateral offer lower interest rates.  
- Paying full EMIs during the moratorium period reduces total interest burden.  
- Shorter loan tenure decreases overall interest paid despite higher EMIs.  
- Interest on education loans is fully deductible under Section 80(E) with no limit.  
- Use loan calculators to plan affordable EMIs and tenure.
