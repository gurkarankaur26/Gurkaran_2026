CONTENT:  
ICICI Bank’s International Banking Unit (IBU) at GIFT City offers Fixed Deposits, Current Accounts, and Savings Accounts in multiple specified foreign currencies including USD, EUR, GBP, CAD, HKD, SGD, AED, and AUD. These accounts cater to Resident Indians, Non-Resident Indians (NRIs), Overseas Citizens of India (OCIs), and foreign nationals, providing access to offshore banking and global investment opportunities.

**Account Opening and Eligibility:**  
- To open an account, customers must connect with their ICICI IBU Relationship Manager or visit the nearest ICICI Bank branch.  
- PAN card proof is mandatory for NRIs and Resident Indians opening savings or current accounts. OCIs can submit Form 60 if PAN is unavailable.  
- Valid ID and address proofs (Officially Valid Documents - OVDs) include:  
  1. Passport  
  2. Voter’s Identity Card  
  3. Driving License  
  4. Aadhaar Card  
  5. NREGA Job Card  
  6. Letter from National Population Register  
- The GIFT City Savings Account must be funded within 30 days of opening. If unfunded for over 90 days, the account may be frozen or closed.  

**Account Features:**  
- No minimum balance requirement for Savings or Current Accounts for Resident Indians, NRIs, and OCIs at the IBU branch.  
- Nomination facility is available.  
- Net Banking facility is provided at the time of account opening, offering convenient online access and management.  
- Demand draft facility is not available at the ICICI IBU branch.  

**Investment and Tax Benefits:**  
- ICICI Bank customers can avail tax incentives applicable to IFSC Banking Units, including exemptions or concessions on certain income and investment activities.  
- Resident Indians may invest in international equities, fractional shares, and global investment products through the IBU, subject to regulatory limits such as the Liberalised Remittance Scheme (LRS).  

**Fund Transfers:**  
- Funds can be transferred conveniently using various methods under the LRS and other applicable regulations.  
- Any received, realised, unspent, or unused foreign exchange from onshore India can be managed through the IBU accounts.
