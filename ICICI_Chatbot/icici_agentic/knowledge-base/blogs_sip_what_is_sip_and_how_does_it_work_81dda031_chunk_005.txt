CONTENT:  
A Systematic Investment Plan (SIP) is a disciplined investment method where you invest a fixed amount at regular intervals in mutual funds. SIPs help you compound your money over time, making them ideal for long-term financial goals such as buying a house or retirement planning.

**Key Features and Benefits:**  
- **Flexibility:** You can modify or cancel your SIP amount, date, or fund choice anytime through the iMobile App or internet banking without any charges or penalties.  
- **Missed Payments:** If a payment is missed due to insufficient balance, no units are allotted for that month, but the SIP continues in the next cycle. Repeated missed payments may lead to cancellation, so ensure funds are available on the debit date.  
- **Returns:** Returns are reflected daily based on the Net Asset Value (NAV) of the mutual fund. Actual gains are realized only upon redemption of units. SIPs grow gradually and benefit from compounding over the long term.  
- **Diversification:** You can start multiple SIPs across various mutual fund schemes to spread risk and balance your portfolio according to your financial goals, risk tolerance, and investment horizon.  
- **Comparison with Lump Sum:** SIP involves investing small amounts regularly, while lump sum investing involves a large one-time investment. The choice depends on your financial goals and risk appetite.  
- **Risk and Returns:** Returns depend on market performance and vary based on the type of fund and asset allocation. SIPs encourage disciplined investing and help mitigate market volatility through rupee cost averaging.  
- **Tax Benefits:** Tax benefits are available only if the SIP is invested in eligible schemes such as Equity Linked Savings Scheme (ELSS), subject to prevailing tax laws.  
- **Long-Term Advantage:** Starting early and investing consistently over a longer period maximizes the benefits of compounding. You can start with a low amount and increase investments gradually.  
- **SIP Calculator:** ICICI Bank provides a SIP calculator to help you estimate potential returns on your investments.

KEY POINTS:  
- SIP allows fixed amount investments at regular intervals in mutual funds.  
- No charges for modifying or cancelling SIPs via iMobile App or internet banking.  
- Missed payments do not allot units but SIP continues; repeated misses may cancel SIP.  
- Returns depend on NAV and are realized on redemption.  
- Multiple SIPs can be started for diversification.  
- Tax benefits available only on eligible schemes like ELSS.  
- SIPs are suitable for long-term goals and disciplined investing.  
- SIP calculator available for return estimation.
