Invesco India Mutual Fund Schemes Overview

SOURCE:

CATEGORY:

TAGS:

CONTENT:  
This document provides an overview of various Invesco India mutual fund schemes available for investment through ICICI Bank. The schemes include Corporate Bond Funds, Liquid Funds, and Low Duration Funds, each offering multiple plan options such as Direct, Regular, Growth, Dividend (IDCW - Income Distribution cum Capital Withdrawal), Monthly, Quarterly, Weekly, and Daily dividend options.

Key Mutual Fund Schemes and Options:

1. **Invesco India Corporate Bond Fund**  
   - Available in Direct and Discretionary plans.  
   - Dividend options: Annual IDCW, Monthly IDCW, Quarterly IDCW, Discretionary IDCW.  
   - Growth option available.  
   - Suitable for investors seeking stable income through corporate bonds.

2. **Invesco India Liquid Fund**  
   - Available in Direct and Regular plans.  
   - Dividend options: Daily IDCW, Weekly IDCW, Monthly IDCW.  
   - Growth option available.  
   - Designed for investors looking for liquidity and low risk.

3. **Invesco India Low Duration Fund**  
   - Available in Direct, Discretionary, and Regular plans.  
   - Dividend options: Daily IDCW, Weekly IDCW, Monthly IDCW, Discretionary IDCW.  
   - Growth option available.  
   - Suitable for investors seeking short-term debt investment with moderate risk.

Each scheme is identified by a unique code (e.g., 106179 for Invesco India Corporate Bond Fund - Ann IDCW). Investors can choose the plan and dividend option that best suits their investment goals and risk appetite.

KEY POINTS:  
- Multiple Invesco India mutual fund schemes are available via ICICI Bank.  
- Schemes include Corporate Bond Fund, Liquid Fund, and Low Duration Fund.  
- Plans offered: Direct, Regular, and Discretionary.  
- Dividend payout options: Annual, Monthly, Quarterly, Weekly, Daily IDCW, and Growth.  
- Each scheme has a unique identification code for easy reference.  
- Suitable for investors with varying risk profiles and investment horizons.  
- Enables diversification across debt and liquid assets.
