CONTENT:  
Credit Cards in India have varying minimum salary requirements depending on the card type and applicant profile. Banks, including ICICI Bank, assess income to determine repayment capacity and creditworthiness. Apart from income, factors such as age, credit score, repayment history, and employment stability also influence eligibility.

**Eligibility Criteria for ICICI Bank Credit Cards:**  
- **Age:** Minimum 21 years (varies by card)  
- **Income:** Regular source of income required. Typically, a net annual income of Rs 2.5 lakh is considered, but some cards have stricter income criteria.  
- **Nationality:** Available to Indian residents and non-residents (subject to confirmation).

**Additional Notes:**  
- Actual eligibility depends on internal policies, credit score, and other criteria.  
- Having a lower income may limit card options but does not necessarily disqualify applicants if other criteria are met.  

KEY POINTS:  
- Minimum age for ICICI Bank credit card applicants is generally 21 years.  
- Minimum net annual income typically starts at Rs 2.5 lakh for credit card eligibility.  
- Income requirements vary by card type, from ₹20,000 monthly for entry-level cards to ₹1.5 lakh and above for super premium cards.  
- Credit score, repayment history, and employment stability are also important eligibility factors.  
- ICICI Bank credit cards are available to both residents and non-residents of India.  
- Card examples provided for each income segment help applicants identify suitable options.
