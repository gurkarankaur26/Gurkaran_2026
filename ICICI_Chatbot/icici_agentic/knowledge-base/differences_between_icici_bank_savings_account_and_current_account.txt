TITLE: Differences Between ICICI Bank Savings Account and Current Account  
SOURCE: ICICI Bank  
CATEGORY: Banking Accounts  
TAGS: Savings Account, Current Account, Minimum Balance, Transaction Limits, Overdraft, Business Banking, Interest Rates  

CONTENT:  
ICICI Bank offers two primary types of accounts catering to different customer needs: Savings Accounts and Current Accounts. Understanding their features, benefits, and eligibility can help customers choose the right account for their financial requirements.

**Savings Account:**  
- Designed for individuals who want to save money and earn interest over time.  
- Helps build an emergency fund or meet short-term financial goals.  
- Ideal for salaried individuals or those with regular income.  
- Offers interest on the deposited amount, helping grow savings.  
- Comes with a monthly transaction limit.  
- Requires maintaining a comparatively low minimum balance, making it affordable.  
- Provides easy access to funds through ATMs, internet banking, and mobile banking.  
- Encourages financial discipline and saving habits.  
- Does not offer overdraft facilities.  

**Current Account:**  
- Specifically designed for businesses, professionals, and individuals who need to carry out frequent and high-volume transactions.  
- Does not earn interest on the balance.  
- Allows unlimited transactions, making it suitable for daily business operations.  
- Offers overdraft facilities to help manage short-term cash flow shortages.  
- Provides cheque book facilities and card payment options to facilitate business payments.  
- Requires maintaining a higher minimum balance compared to Savings Accounts.  
- Supports higher transaction limits for deposits and withdrawals.  
- Helps businesses manage sudden expenses by allowing overdrafts up to a certain limit.  

**Key Differences:**  
- **Interest:** Savings Accounts earn interest; Current Accounts do not.  
- **Transaction Limits:** Savings Accounts have lower transaction limits; Current Accounts allow unlimited transactions.  
- **Minimum Balance:** Savings Accounts require a lower minimum balance; Current Accounts require a higher minimum balance.  
- **Overdraft Facility:** Available only with Current Accounts.  
- **Purpose:** Savings Accounts are for personal savings and financial discipline; Current Accounts are for business transactions and cash flow management.  

**Switching Between Accounts:**  
- Direct switching from a Current Account to a Savings Account is not possible due to different regulatory norms and purposes.  
- To move from a Current Account to a Savings Account, customers must open a new Savings Account and close the Current Account.  
- This process can be done by visiting the nearest ICICI Bank branch, where bank representatives will assist.  

KEY POINTS:  
- Savings Accounts are suitable for individuals seeking to save and earn interest with limited transactions.  
- Current Accounts cater to businesses requiring frequent, high-volume transactions and overdraft facilities.  
- Minimum balance requirements differ: lower for Savings, higher for Current Accounts.  
- Overdraft facility is exclusive to Current Accounts.  
- No direct switching between Current and Savings Accounts; a new account must be opened.
