CONTENT:  
ICICI Bank provides a range of tailored financial products for companies, including those with ESG credentials, subject to internal eligibility and validation. Businesses are advised to maintain account opening forms, KYC documents, transaction authorisations, mandate changes, reconciliation files, and correspondence with ICICI Bank for statutory audits and compliance checks.

The bank assists with reconciliation data, furnishes payment proofs, and facilitates chargeback or routing enquiries. However, any contractual disputes between business parties must be resolved privately or through legal channels.

ICICI Bank offers various financing options linked to current-account relationships for seamless repayment and overdraft management. These include working-capital finance, invoice discounting, trade finance, and term loans.

Large or strategically-rated customers may benefit from pre-approved limits or overdraft facilities based on historical cash flows and account conduct, which helps reduce turnaround time for urgent funding needs.

Interest rates on these facilities are determined by the business’s credit profile, collateral, loan tenor, and ICICI Bank’s internal pricing policies. Rates may be fixed or floating and are tied to benchmark lending rates.

KEY POINTS:  
- Financial products available: working-capital finance, invoice discounting, trade finance, term loans  
- Facilities can be linked to current accounts for easy repayment and overdraft management  
- Pre-approved limits or overdraft facilities available for large or strategically-rated customers  
- Interest rates depend on credit profile, collateral, tenor, and internal pricing; fixed or floating rates tied to benchmark lending rates  
- Businesses must keep all relevant documentation for audits and compliance  
- ICICI Bank supports reconciliation data, payment proofs, and chargeback enquiries  
- Contractual disputes between business parties are not handled by the bank
