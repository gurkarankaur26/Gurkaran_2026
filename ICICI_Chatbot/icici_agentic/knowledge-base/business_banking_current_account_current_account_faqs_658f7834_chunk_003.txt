TITLE:  
ICICI Bank Current Accounts – Features, Eligibility, Charges, and Services

SOURCE:  
ICICI Bank

CATEGORY:  
Banking – Current Accounts

TAGS:  
Current Account, Business Banking, Fees, Charges, Eligibility, Documentation, Foreign Entities, Merchant Services, Remittances, Sweep-in FD, Cheque Book, Stop Payment

CONTENT:  
ICICI Bank offers a range of current account variants tailored to meet the needs of businesses, government entities, foreign entities, and specialized sectors such as government e-marketplace (GEM) sellers, RERA-linked collections, and project developers requiring escrow accounts.

**Eligibility and Documentation:**  
- Businesses must provide registration certificates, trust deed, PAN card, and authorised signatory documents.  
- Foreign entities with Indian branches or permitted operations can open current accounts subject to regulatory approvals and documentation compliance.  
- Applications can be made online via the ICICI Bank website or by visiting a branch, where bank officials assist with documentation, KYC, and product selection.

**Charges and Fees:**  
- Monthly account maintenance fees may apply depending on the current account variant.  
- Charges for cheque book issuance, cash deposits beyond free limits, outward remittances, NEFT/RTGS transactions at branches, merchant services, and penalties for returned cheques are applicable as per the Schedule of Charges.  
- Free cash deposit limits vary by account variant and turnover band; exceeding these limits incurs per-transaction charges.  
- Stop-payment requests generally attract nominal fees, with charges varying by request channel (branch, online, or relationship manager).  
- Locker allotment, safety deposit services, and allied trade services have separate fee structures and are optional.

**Interest and Earnings:**  
- Current accounts typically do not earn interest like savings accounts.  
- Some variants offer linked deposit sweep features that allow surplus funds to earn interest via sweep-in fixed deposit facilities.

**Merchant and Remittance Services:**  
- Merchant acquiring fees, gateway integration fees, transaction discount rates (TDR), and POS terminal rentals apply. Rates are negotiable based on transaction volumes and merchant verticals.  
- Foreign exchange rates are competitive, and remittance charges may be reduced or waived for business customers depending on relationship depth and product bundles.

**Returns and Penalties:**  
- Returns due to insufficient funds or mismatched signatures attract return charges and may impact account standing.  
- Customers are informed of such charges in the account terms and Schedule of Charges.

**Additional Services:**  
- Demand drafts and banker’s cheques are charged as per the Schedule of Charges.  
- Relationship Managers provide personalized assistance for account management and service needs.

KEY POINTS:  
- Multiple current account variants tailored for businesses, government, foreign entities, and specialized sectors.  
- Mandatory documentation includes registration certificates, PAN, trust deed, and authorised signatory proof.  
- Charges include maintenance fees, transaction fees, cheque book fees, remittance fees, merchant service fees, and penalties.  
- Free cash deposit limits vary; exceeding limits incurs charges.  
- No interest on current accounts, but sweep-in FD linked accounts available for earnings on surplus funds.  
- Merchant acquiring and remittance fees are negotiable based on relationship and volume.  
- Stop-payment and returned cheque charges apply as per Schedule of Charges.  
- Optional locker and safety deposit services available with separate fees.  
- Applications can be made online or at branches with support from Relationship Managers.
