CONTENT:  
Credit Cards offer benefits such as short-term credit, reward points, discounts, and safer shopping experiences. With rising usage in India, understanding the eligibility criteria for ICICI Bank Credit Cards is essential before applying.

**Eligibility Criteria:**  
- **Age:** Applicants must be between 21 years and 100 years of age.  
- **Income:** Minimum annual income typically ranges from ₹2.5 lakh to ₹3 lakh, depending on the card type.  
- **Credit Score:** A CIBIL score above 750 is preferred for smoother approval.  
- **Employment & Income Stability:** Proof of stable income and employment is required to assess repayment capacity.  

**Required Documents:**  
- **KYC Proof:** Aadhaar Card, Passport, Voter ID, or Driving Licence.  
- **Address Proof:** Utility bills (electricity, water, gas), Passport, or Aadhaar Card.  
- **Income Proof:** Salary slips, latest bank statements, or tax returns.  
- **Identity Proof:** PAN Card or Form 60.  
- **Photographs:** Recent passport-sized photographs.  
- **Additional Documents:** Depending on the card type, business registration certificates or financial statements may be required.  

**Application Process:**  
- Submit complete and accurate information along with the required documents.  
- The bank conducts a thorough background check including credit score and income verification.  
- Approval depends on meeting eligibility criteria and document verification.  

**Reasons for Rejection:**  
- Low credit score.  
- Insufficient income.  
- High existing debt.  
- Incomplete or inaccurate documents.  
- Multiple recent credit card applications.  

KEY POINTS:  
- Age eligibility: 21 to 100 years.  
- Minimum income: ₹2.5 lakh to ₹3 lakh annually.  
- Preferred credit score: Above 750.  
- Essential documents: KYC, address proof, income proof, PAN/Form 60, photographs.  
- Additional documents may be required based on card type.  
- Complete and accurate documentation is critical for approval.  
- Applications can be rejected due to low credit score, insufficient income, or incomplete documents.
