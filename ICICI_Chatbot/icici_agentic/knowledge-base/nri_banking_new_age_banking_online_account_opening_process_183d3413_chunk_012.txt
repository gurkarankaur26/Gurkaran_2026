ICICI Bank NRI Fund Transfer and Withdrawal Services

SOURCE:  
ICICI Bank

CATEGORY:  
NRI Banking, Fund Transfer, Account Management

TAGS:  
NRI, NRE Account, NRO Account, Fund Transfer, Wire Transfer, Money2India, Exchange Houses, Foreign Currency Cheques, INR Cheques, Net Banking, RTGS, Remittance

CONTENT:  
ICICI Bank offers multiple convenient modes for Non-Resident Indians (NRIs) to transfer and withdraw funds from their NRE (Non-Resident External) and NRO (Non-Resident Ordinary) accounts.

**Money Transfer Options**

1. **Online Modes**  
- **Money2India**: An internet-based money transfer tracking service available only in Canada, Hong Kong, Singapore, Sweden, Switzerland, UK, USA, and UAE.  
- Features: Anytime, anywhere access, online tracking of funds, competitive exchange rates, 24-hour customer care via toll-free phone and email.  
- Registration: One-time instant online registration required at www.money2india.com.

2. **Offline Modes**  
- **Wire Transfer**: Available for most countries, suitable for large value transfers. Initiated by visiting your local bank abroad and providing wire transfer instructions.  
- **Exchange House Transfers**: ICICI Bank partners with 24 key Exchange houses and Correspondent banks in Gulf Cooperation Council (GCC) countries (Bahrain, Kuwait, Qatar, Saudi Arabia, Oman, UAE) offering Insta-Transfers, I-Express, Speed Remittance, and INR Demand Draft (DD) facilities.  
- **Foreign Currency Cheques**: Cheques drawn on foreign banks in foreign currency can be deposited into the beneficiary’s NRE Savings Account.  
- **INR Cheques**:  
  - INR cheques can be drawn from other NRE Savings Accounts in favor of your ICICI Bank NRE Savings Account.  
  - INR cheques can be drawn from any account in favor of your ICICI Bank NRO Savings Account.

**Withdrawal and Payment from NRE/NRO Accounts**

You can make payments to individuals and companies in India through the following channels:

1. **Net Banking**  
- Recommended for convenience and speed.  
- For high-value transactions, use the RTGS (Real Time Gross Settlement) facility.

2. **Cheque**  
- Issue cheques from your NRI account (NRE or NRO) for payments.

3. **Branch Visit**  
- Visit the nearest ICICI Bank branch in India to place a request for fund transfer.

**Permitted Transfers to Individuals**

| Sending From | Sending To | Permitted Channels | Notes |
|--------------|------------|--------------------|-------|
| NRE Account  | NRE Account | Net Banking, Cheque, Through Branch | Allowed |
| NRE Account  | NRO Account | Net Banking, Cheque, Through Branch | Allowed |
| NRE Account  | Resident   | Net Banking, Cheque, Through Branch | Allowed |
| NRO Account  | NRE Account | Restricted (Refer to NRO to NRE Transfers policy) | Limited |
| NRO Account  | NRO Account | Net Banking, Cheque, Through Branch | Allowed |
| NRO Account  | Resident   | Net Banking, Cheque, Through Branch | Allowed |
| Resident     | NRE Account | Not Permitted | Not Allowed |
| Resident     | NRO Account | Net Banking, Cheque, Through Branch | Allowed |

**Payments to Companies in India**

- From ICICI NRO/NRE account to INR account of a company:  
  - Use Bill Payment through Net Banking.  
  - Process: Login to Net Banking -> Bill Payment section -> Select company and pay.

**Important Notes**

- Some transfer modes may not be available depending on your country of residence.  
- GCC countries include Bahrain, Kuwait, Qatar, Saudi Arabia, Oman, and UAE.

KEY POINTS:  
- Money2India is an online money transfer and tracking service available in select countries with 24-hour customer support.  
- Wire transfers and exchange house transfers are available offline for large and quick remittances.  
- Foreign currency cheques can be deposited into NRE accounts; INR cheques can be drawn in favor of NRE/NRO accounts.  
- Payments and withdrawals can be made via Net Banking, Cheque, or branch visit.  
- RTGS is recommended for high-value transactions.  
- Transfers from NRE to Resident accounts are permitted; transfers from Resident to NRE accounts are not permitted.  
- Bill payments to companies in India can be done via Net Banking from NRE/NRO accounts.  
- Availability of transfer modes depends on the country of residence.  
- GCC countries have special exchange house tie-ups for remittance.
