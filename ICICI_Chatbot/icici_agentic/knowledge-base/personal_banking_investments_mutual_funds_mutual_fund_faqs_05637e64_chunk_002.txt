CONTENT:  
Mutual funds are investment vehicles pooling money from multiple investors to invest in diversified portfolios of securities. They are broadly classified into the following categories:

1. **Equity Funds**  
   - Invest primarily in equity and equity-related instruments.  
   - Aim for long-term capital growth.

2. **Debt Funds**  
   - Invest in fixed-income securities such as government/corporate bonds, treasury bills, and money market instruments.  
   - Provide reliable income, liquidity, and diversification.

3. **Hybrid Funds**  
   - Invest in a combination of debt and equity instruments.  
   - Offer benefits of asset allocation and diversification.  
   - Tailored for specific financial goals like retirement and child’s education.

4. **Index Funds/ETFs and Fund of Funds (FoF)**  
   - Invest in market indices like Sensex or Nifty 50.  
   - Suitable for investors who want to replicate market index allocation.

**Risks Involved:**  
- Market risk  
- Interest rate risk  
- Credit risk  
- Liquidity risk  

Diversification, expert fund management, and regulatory oversight help mitigate these risks. Investors should choose schemes aligned with their financial goals and risk tolerance.

**KYC (Know Your Customer) Requirements:**  
- PAN card (mandatory for all investors except certain government bodies or SEBI-notified exemptions)  
- Proof of Identity (Aadhaar card)  
- Photograph  
- KYC form and FATCA-CRS declaration  

For non-individual investors, additional documents may be required as per the KYC form.

**KYC Compliance:**  
- KYC is mandatory as per SEBI regulations to invest in mutual funds.  
- It verifies identity, address, and other details once for all market intermediaries.  
- Joint investments require each holder to complete KYC individually, complying with PAN, KYC, and FATCA norms.

**KYC Status and Impact on Mutual Fund Transactions:**  

| KYC Status    | When Applicable                         | Impact on MF Investment                                      | Re-KYC Required?                  |  
|---------------|---------------------------------------|--------------------------------------------------------------|----------------------------------|  
| KYC Validated | Original KYC based on Aadhaar          | Can transact across all fund houses                           | No                               |  
| KYC Registered| Original KYC based on Official Valid Documents (OVD) except Aadhaar | No impact on existing investments; cannot invest in new fund houses | Yes, to invest in new fund houses |  
| On Hold       | Original KYC based on non-OVD          | Debarred from all mutual fund transactions                    | Yes                              |  
| Rejected     | After KYC "On Hold" status for 10-15 days | Debarred from all mutual fund transactions                    | Yes                              |  

**Eligible Investors:**  
- Resident individuals  
- Non-Resident Indians (NRIs)  
- Resident minors (through guardians)  
- Hindu Undivided Families (HUFs)  
- Companies, partnerships, LLPs  
- Trusts and other SEBI-permitted entities  

All must complete KYC and onboarding registration to invest via ICICI Bank’s platform.

KEY POINTS:  
- Mutual funds are classified into Equity, Debt, Hybrid, and Index funds.  
- Each category serves different investment goals and risk profiles.  
- KYC is mandatory for all investors to comply with SEBI regulations.  
- PAN is mandatory except for specific exemptions.  
- Joint investors must individually complete KYC and PAN compliance.  
- KYC status affects the ability to transact in mutual funds.  
- ICICI Bank facilitates mutual fund investments for a wide range of investor categories.  
- Risks include market, interest rate, credit, and liquidity risks, mitigated by diversification and expert management.
