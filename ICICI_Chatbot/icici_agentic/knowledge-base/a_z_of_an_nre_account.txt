TITLE: A-Z of an NRE Account  
SOURCE: ICICI Bank  
CATEGORY: NRI Banking / NRE Account  
TAGS: NRE Account, NRI Banking, Fixed Deposit, Recurring Deposit, Repatriation, Tax Benefits, FEMA Regulations  

CONTENT:  
A Non-Resident External (NRE) account is a bank account designed for Non-Resident Indians (NRIs), Persons of Indian Origin (PIOs), and Overseas Citizens of India (OCIs) to manage their foreign earned income in India. The account is denominated in Indian Rupees (₹), with foreign currency deposits automatically converted to INR at prevailing exchange rates. There is no upper limit on the balance maintained in an NRE account.

**Key Features and Benefits:**  
- **Repatriability:** Both principal and interest amounts in the NRE account can be freely repatriated abroad without any upper limit.  
- **Tax Benefits:** Interest earned on the NRE account is completely exempt from income tax in India.  
- **Account Types:** Apart from the savings account, NRIs can open NRE Fixed Deposit (FD) and Recurring Deposit (RD) accounts with tenures ranging from 1 to 10 years (tenure may vary by bank). Upon maturity, the principal and interest amounts are credited back to the NRE savings account and are freely repatriable.  
- **Eligibility:** Available to NRIs, PIOs, and OCIs. NRIs who qualify as Indian residents under FEMA regulations must close their NRE accounts.  

**Permissible Transactions (as per FEMA Regulations 2016):**  
- **Credits:**  
  - Remittances from overseas accounts or transfers from NRE/Foreign Currency Non-Resident (FCNR) accounts.  
  - Funds from NRO accounts up to USD 1 million per financial year (applicable on capital income such as maturity proceeds of FD, sale of property, redemption of mutual funds and shares).  
- **Debits:**  
  - Payments for local Indian expenses, investments, and transfers abroad without restrictions.  

**Additional Information:**  
- The NRE account helps NRIs send money to family in India, manage local expenses, and invest in India using foreign income.  
- The account is maintained in Indian Rupees, providing convenience and protection against exchange rate fluctuations for funds held in India.  

KEY POINTS:  
- NRE account is for NRIs, PIOs, and OCIs to hold foreign income in INR.  
- No upper limit on balance maintained.  
- Interest earned is tax-free in India.  
- Funds (principal + interest) are fully repatriable without limit.  
- NRE FD and RD available with 1 to 10 years tenure.  
- Funds can be credited from overseas remittances, NRE/FCNR accounts, and NRO accounts (up to USD 1 million).  
- NRIs qualifying as Indian residents must close NRE accounts as per FEMA.
