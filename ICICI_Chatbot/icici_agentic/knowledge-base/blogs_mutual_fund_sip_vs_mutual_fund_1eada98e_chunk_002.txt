CONTENT:  
Section 80C of the Income Tax Act allows you to claim deductions of up to ₹1,50,000 in a financial year by investing in eligible instruments, including mutual funds.

You can invest in mutual funds through ICICI Bank in two ways: Systematic Investment Plan (SIP) or lump sum investment. To start investing, ensure you have a Demat Account with ICICI Bank. Complete your KYC online via the ICICI Bank website before choosing a mutual fund scheme that aligns with your financial goals and risk appetite.

- **SIP Investment:**  
  - Invest small, manageable amounts regularly (monthly or quarterly).  
  - Ideal for investors with low or regular income.  
  - Helps build investment discipline and gradually grow wealth.  
  - Suitable for long-term goals such as retirement, education, or buying a home.  
  - Benefits from compounding and better handles market volatility.

- **Lump Sum Investment:**  
  - Invest a one-time amount directly into a mutual fund scheme.  
  - Requires monitoring to track performance and alignment with goals.

Mutual funds provide diversification by investing across various sectors, stocks, and asset classes, reducing overall risk and increasing the potential for steady returns.

SIP is a method of investing in mutual funds and is complementary to lump sum investments. Your choice between SIP and lump sum depends on your financial goals, risk tolerance, investment horizon, and cash flow preferences.

KEY POINTS:  
- Section 80C deduction limit: ₹1,50,000 per financial year.  
- Investment modes: SIP (regular small investments) and lump sum (one-time investment).  
- Requirement: ICICI Bank Demat Account and completed KYC.  
- SIP benefits: disciplined investing, compounding, risk mitigation, suitable for long-term goals.  
- Mutual funds offer diversification across sectors and asset classes.  
- Regular monitoring of investments is essential to stay aligned with financial goals.
