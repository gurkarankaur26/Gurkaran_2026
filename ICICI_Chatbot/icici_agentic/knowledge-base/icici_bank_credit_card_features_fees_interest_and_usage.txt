TITLE: ICICI Bank Credit Card - Features, Fees, Interest, and Usage  
SOURCE: ICICI Bank  
CATEGORY: Credit Cards  
TAGS: Credit Card, Fees, Interest Rates, Rewards, International Usage, Payment, Charges, Eligibility  

CONTENT:  
ICICI Bank Credit Cards offer a range of benefits including reward points that accumulate with usage. These points can be redeemed for gift vouchers, merchandise, travel bookings, or statement credit depending on the card’s rewards program.

**Cash Withdrawal:**  
Cash advances are possible at ATMs but attract interest and transaction fees from the date of the transaction. There is no interest-free period on cash withdrawals.

**Repayment Options:**  
Revolving credit allows you to pay only the minimum amount due and carry forward the remaining balance to the next billing cycle. Interest is charged on the unpaid portion until fully repaid.

**International Usage:**  
Many ICICI Bank Credit Cards support international transactions. Foreign currency mark-up charges apply as per the card’s terms and conditions. The transaction amount is converted into INR using the card network’s exchange rate, and a forex markup fee (a percentage of the transaction value) is added.

**Fees:**  
- **Joining Fee:** A one-time charge applied when the card is issued. Often accompanied by welcome benefits such as reward points or vouchers.  
- **Annual Fee:** A recurring yearly charge for maintaining the card and its benefits. Some cards offer annual fee waivers if you meet specified spending thresholds.  
- **Late Payment Fee:** Charged if the minimum or total amount due is not paid by the due date. The fee varies based on the outstanding balance and card terms.  
- **Finance Charges:** Interest applied on unpaid balances, cash advances, EMI conversions, and revolving credit amounts until fully repaid. Interest starts accruing from the due date for unpaid balances and from the transaction date for cash advances.  
- **GST:** Applicable on fees, interest, and other service charges as per prevailing tax regulations.

**Impact of Late or Missed Payments:**  
Interest continues to accrue on outstanding balances until cleared. New purchases may also attract interest until dues are fully paid. Consistent delays or missed payments can negatively affect your credit history and credit score.

KEY POINTS:  
- Reward points can be redeemed for various benefits.  
- Cash advances attract immediate interest and fees.  
- Revolving credit allows minimum payments with interest on unpaid amounts.  
- International transactions incur forex markup fees and currency conversion.  
- Joining and annual fees apply; some cards offer fee waivers on meeting spending criteria.  
- Late payment fees and finance charges apply on unpaid dues.  
- GST is applicable on all fees and charges.  
- Late or missed payments impact credit score negatively.
