TITLE: ICICI Bank Mutual Fund Schemes Overview  
SOURCE: ICICI Bank  
CATEGORY: Mutual Funds  
TAGS: Mutual Funds, Debt Funds, Liquid Funds, Corporate Bond Funds, Gilt Funds, Investment Options  

CONTENT:  
This document provides a comprehensive list of various mutual fund schemes available through ICICI Bank, focusing on debt and liquid funds from multiple fund houses such as SBI, Sundaram, and Tata. These schemes include growth options and income distribution cum capital withdrawal (IDCW) plans with different payout frequencies (weekly, monthly, quarterly, periodic).  

Key mutual fund categories and schemes include:  

1. **SBI Short Term Debt Fund**  
   - Variants: Regular and Weekly IDCW options  
   - Suitable for investors seeking short-term debt investment with periodic income.  

2. **Sundaram Banking & PSU Fund**  
   - Growth options available in Direct and Regular plans.  
   - Focuses on banking and public sector undertakings (PSU) bonds.  

3. **Sundaram Corporate Bond Fund**  
   - Growth options in Direct and Regular plans.  
   - Invests primarily in corporate bonds for stable returns.  

4. **Sundaram Liquid Fund**  
   - Multiple IDCW options (Monthly) and Growth plans.  
   - Ideal for investors seeking liquidity with moderate returns.  

5. **Sundaram Low Duration Fund**  
   - Growth options available.  
   - Targets low duration debt instruments for short-term investment.  

6. **Sundaram Money Market Fund**  
   - Growth and Monthly IDCW options in Direct and Regular plans.  
   - Invests in money market instruments for liquidity and safety.  

7. **Sundaram Ultra Short Duration Fund**  
   - Growth and Monthly IDCW options.  
   - Suitable for ultra-short-term investment horizons.  

8. **Tata Corporate Bond Fund**  
   - Growth and IDCW options (Monthly, Quarterly, Periodic) in Direct and Regular plans.  
   - Focuses on corporate bonds with varying income distribution frequencies.  

9. **Tata Gilt Securities Fund**  
   - Growth and IDCW options in Regular plans.  
   - Invests in government securities (gilts) for safety and steady returns.  

KEY POINTS:  
- Mutual fund schemes are available in both Direct and Regular plans.  
- Income Distribution cum Capital Withdrawal (IDCW) options provide periodic income with frequencies including weekly, monthly, quarterly, and periodic payouts.  
- Growth options allow capital appreciation without periodic income distribution.  
- Funds cover a range of debt instruments including short-term debt, corporate bonds, money market instruments, and gilt securities.  
- Suitable for investors with varying risk appetites and investment horizons seeking income or growth.
