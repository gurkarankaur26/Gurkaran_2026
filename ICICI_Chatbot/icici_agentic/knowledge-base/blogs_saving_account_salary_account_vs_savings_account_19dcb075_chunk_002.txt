TITLE:  
Difference Between Salary Account and Savings Account

SOURCE: ICICI Bank

CATEGORY:  
Banking Accounts

TAGS:  
Salary Account, Savings Account, Interest Rates, Debit Card, Fund Transfer, ATM Access, Digital Banking

CONTENT:  
Salary accounts and savings accounts are both deposit accounts designed to securely manage funds, but they serve different purposes and have distinct features. Both account types provide digital banking access through net banking and mobile banking, issue debit cards for transactions and withdrawals, support online fund transfers and bill payments, and allow ATM access. Additionally, both accounts earn interest on the available balance and are governed by standard banking policies and regulations.

A salary account is primarily used to receive salary credits and other electronic payments directly from an employer. In contrast, a savings account is generally used for saving money and managing day-to-day expenses, and it can receive electronic fund transfers from various sources.

Most individuals maintain both a salary account and a savings account, which can be held with the same or different banks. It is important to compare interest rates and other features before selecting a bank for either account type to ensure the best benefits.

KEY POINTS:  
- Both salary and savings accounts are deposit accounts for securely managing funds.  
- Digital banking access is available for both account types via net banking and mobile banking.  
- Debit cards are issued for transactions and ATM withdrawals in both accounts.  
- Both accounts support online fund transfers and bill payments.  
- Interest is earned on the available balance in both accounts.  
- Salary accounts receive salary and other electronic credits; savings accounts receive electronic fund transfers.  
- Both accounts are governed by standard banking regulations.  
- Individuals often maintain both accounts for salary receipt and savings management.  
- Compare interest rates and features before choosing a bank for these accounts.
