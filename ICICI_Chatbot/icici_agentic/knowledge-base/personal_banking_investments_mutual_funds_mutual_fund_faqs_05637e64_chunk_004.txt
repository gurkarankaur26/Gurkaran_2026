TITLE:  
Taxation on Capital Gains from Mutual Funds

SOURCE: ICICI Bank  
CATEGORY:  
Mutual Funds, Taxation, Investments

TAGS:  
Capital Gains Tax, Mutual Funds, Equity Mutual Funds, Debt Mutual Funds, Hybrid Funds, Tax Rates, Investment Tenure, STCG, LTCG

CONTENT:  
The tax on capital gains from mutual funds depends on the type of mutual fund, the holding period, and the date of investment and redemption. The classification of short-term and long-term capital gains varies between equity and debt-oriented funds.

**1. Equity Mutual Funds (including Equity Index Funds, Equity ETFs, Equity FoFs):**  
- **Holding Period:**  
  - Short-term: Less than 12 months  
  - Long-term: More than 12 months  
- **Tax Rates (Pre-Budget Redemption up to July 22, 2024):**  
  - Short-term Capital Gains (STCG): 15%  
  - Long-term Capital Gains (LTCG): 10% on gains exceeding ₹1 lakh  
- **Tax Rates (Post-Budget Redemption on or after July 23, 2024):**  
  - STCG: 20%  
  - LTCG: 12.5% on gains exceeding ₹1.25 lakh  
- **Additional Information:** Securities Transaction Tax (STT) is applicable as these funds invest ≥65% in domestic equity shares. Balanced Advantage Funds (BAF) are considered equity funds if they maintain gross equity exposure of 65% or more.

**2. Debt Oriented Funds:**  
- **Holding Period:**  
  - Pre-Budget (Investment before April 1, 2023):  
    - STCG: Less than 36 months  
    - LTCG: More than 36 months  
  - Post-Budget (Investment before April 1, 2023):  
    - STCG: Less than 24 months  
    - LTCG: More than 24 months  
- **Tax Rates:**  
  - Pre-Budget STCG: Taxed as per individual slab rate  
  - Pre-Budget LTCG: 20% with indexation benefit  
  - Post-Budget STCG: Taxed as per slab rate  
  - Post-Budget LTCG: 12.5% without indexation  
- **For investments made on or after April 1, 2023:**  
  - Only STCG applies and is taxed as per slab rate; LTCG is not applicable.

**3. Other Funds (Gold ETFs, Silver ETFs, Fund of Funds, Multi-Asset Allocation Funds, International FoFs, Hybrid Mutual Funds with ≤35% equity):**  
- **Holding Period:**  
  - Short-term: Less than 12 months  
  - Long-term: More than 12 months  
- **Tax Rates:**  
  - STCG: Taxed as per slab rate  
  - LTCG: 12.5%  
- **Investment and Redemption Dates:**  
  - For investments prior to April 1, 2023, and redemption after July 23, 2024, LTCG is taxed at 12.5%.  
  - For investments after April 1, 2023, and redemption on or after April 1, 2025, LTCG is taxed at 12.5%.  
  - For redemption between July 23, 2024, and March 31, 2025, LTCG is not applicable.

**Key Abbreviations:**  
- STCG: Short-Term Capital Gains  
- LTCG: Long-Term Capital Gains  
- ETF: Exchange Traded Fund  
- FoF: Fund of Funds  
- BAF: Balanced Advantage Fund  
- STT: Securities Transaction Tax

KEY POINTS:  
- Equity mutual funds have a 12-month threshold for short-term vs long-term capital gains; debt funds have 24 or 36 months depending on investment date.  
- Post-Budget changes (effective July 23, 2024) have increased tax rates on equity mutual fund gains and altered holding periods for debt funds.  
- STCG on equity funds is taxed at 20% post-budget; LTCG at 12.5% on gains above ₹1.25 lakh.  
- Debt fund LTCG post-budget is taxed at 12.5% without indexation; STCG is taxed as per slab rate.  
- Hybrid funds with less than or equal to 35% equity are taxed like debt funds.  
- STT applies to equity-oriented funds.  
- Indexation benefit is available only for pre-budget debt fund LTCG.
