TITLE:  
Taxation and Investment Process for Direct Mutual Funds

SOURCE: ICICI Bank  
CATEGORY: Mutual Funds, Investment, Taxation  
TAGS: Mutual Funds, Direct Plans, Taxation, Equity Funds, Debt Funds, Investment Process, KYC, Expense Ratio  

CONTENT:  
Taxation of direct mutual funds depends on the type of fund and the holding period. Mutual funds investing 65% or more in equities or related instruments are classified as equity funds, while others are considered debt funds.

**Taxation on Equity Funds:**  
- Short-Term Capital Gains (STCG) tax rate: 15%  
- Long-Term Capital Gains (LTCG) tax rate: 10%  
- LTCG up to ₹1 lakh in a financial year is exempt from tax  

**Taxation on Debt Funds:**  
- Capital gains are taxed according to the investor’s applicable income tax slab  
- Gains are added to the investor’s taxable income  

**Investment Process for Direct Mutual Funds:**  
- Investors can invest directly via the AMC’s (Asset Management Companies) website or through ICICI Bank’s digital platforms such as the iMobile app and Internet Banking.  
- Ensure your KYC (Know Your Customer) is verified before investing.  
- Once KYC is verified, browse through fund options and invest with a few clicks.  
- Direct plans eliminate distributor commissions, resulting in cost savings.  
- It is advisable to compare fund performance, objectives, and expense ratios before investing to make informed decisions.  

**Benefits of Direct Mutual Funds:**  
- Lower expense ratios compared to regular plans  
- Increased transparency  
- No intermediary commissions  
- Suitable for long-term wealth creation  
- Cost-efficient and personalized investment experience  

KEY POINTS:  
- Equity funds require 65%+ investment in equities; others are debt funds  
- STCG on equity funds taxed at 15%; LTCG taxed at 10% with ₹1 lakh exemption  
- Debt fund gains taxed as per individual tax slab  
- Direct mutual funds can be purchased via AMC websites or ICICI Bank digital platforms  
- KYC verification is mandatory before investing  
- Direct plans save on distributor commissions, reducing costs  
- Compare fund performance and expense ratios before investing  
- Direct mutual funds are ideal for long-term wealth creation due to lower costs and transparency
