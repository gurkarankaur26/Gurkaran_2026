TITLE: Portfolio Investment Scheme (PIS) Account for NRIs  
SOURCE: ICICI Bank  
CATEGORY: NRI Banking, Investments  
TAGS: NRI, Portfolio Investment Scheme, PIS Account, Indian Stocks, Equity Investment, NRE Account, Repatriation  

CONTENT:  
The Portfolio Investment Scheme (PIS) enables Non-Resident Indians (NRIs) to invest in equity shares of Indian companies in the secondary market on a repatriation basis. NRIs can invest through an NRE PIS account linked with a registered stockbroker in India. This scheme facilitates the purchase and sale of shares and convertible debentures of Indian companies on recognized stock exchanges.

Key features include:  
- Investment through an NRE PIS account, allowing repatriation of funds.  
- Transactions are routed through a registered stockbroker in India.  
- Auto settlement of secondary market transactions through the PIS account.  

Eligibility:  
- Non-Resident Indians (NRIs) holding an NRE or NRO account with ICICI Bank.  
- Must have a valid PIS permission from the Reserve Bank of India (RBI).  

Process to open a PIS account:  
1. Open an NRE/NRO account with ICICI Bank.  
2. Submit the PIS application form along with necessary KYC documents.  
3. Obtain PIS permission from RBI through the bank.  
4. Link the PIS account with a demat and trading account with a registered stockbroker.  

Benefits:  
- Enables NRIs to invest directly in Indian equity markets.  
- Facilitates repatriation of sale proceeds and dividends.  
- Simplifies compliance with RBI regulations through auto settlement.  
- Access to a wide range of Indian stocks and convertible debentures.  

Fees and Charges:  
- Charges applicable as per the bank’s tariff for PIS account maintenance and transactions.  
- Brokerage fees as per the stockbroker’s schedule.  

FAQs:  
Q: Can NRIs invest in Indian stocks through PIS on a repatriation basis?  
A: Yes, NRIs can invest in Indian equities through an NRE PIS account with repatriation benefits.  

Q: Is RBI approval required for PIS?  
A: Yes, RBI permission is mandatory and facilitated by the bank.  

Q: Can dividends be repatriated?  
A: Yes, dividends received on shares held under PIS can be repatriated.  

KEY POINTS:  
- PIS allows NRIs to invest in Indian equities on repatriation basis.  
- Requires an NRE/NRO account and RBI approval.  
- Transactions done through registered stockbrokers with auto settlement.  
- Dividends and sale proceeds are repatriable.  
- Fees apply as per bank and broker policies.
