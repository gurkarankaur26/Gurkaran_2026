CONTENT:  
ICICI Bank offers a variety of Mutual Fund investment options tailored to different investor needs, risk appetites, and financial goals. These funds are structured to provide flexibility, risk management, and potential returns suitable for both conservative and aggressive investors.

**Types of Mutual Funds by Investment Objective and Structure:**  

1. **Fixed Maturity Funds (FMFs):**  
   - Have a predetermined maturity date, providing a clear investment horizon.  
   - Suitable for investors seeking fixed returns with minimal interest rate risk.  

2. **Pension Funds:**  
   - Designed to build a retirement corpus by investing in a diversified mix of assets.  
   - Ideal for individuals planning for a secure post-retirement financial future.  

3. **Mutual Fund Structures:**  
   - **Open-ended Funds:** Allow continuous buying and selling of units, offering liquidity and flexibility. Suitable for long-term wealth creation.  
   - **Closed-ended Funds:** Have a fixed maturity period and limited units. Units can be bought only during the initial offer period and traded on stock exchanges. Suitable for long-term investments with potential tax benefits.  
   - **Interval Funds:** Combine features of open and closed-ended funds, allowing redemption at predetermined intervals. Suitable for investors seeking a balance between liquidity and long-term investment.  

**Risk Categories of Mutual Funds:**  

- **Very Low Risk Funds:**  
  - Example: Money Market Funds.  
  - Invest primarily in low-risk securities.  
  - Ideal for conservative investors focused on capital preservation and minimal value fluctuations.  

- **Low Risk Funds:**  
  - Example: Government Bond Funds.  
  - Aim for income generation with slightly higher risk than very low risk funds.  
  - Suitable for investors with moderate risk tolerance.  

- **Medium Risk Funds:**  
  - Example: Balanced Funds (combining equity and debt).  
  - Offer a balance between risk and reward.  
  - Suitable for investors seeking moderate growth with manageable risk.  

- **High Risk Funds:**  
  - Example: Sector-specific Equity Funds.  
  - Focus on capital appreciation with higher volatility.  
  - Suitable for investors willing to accept higher risk for potentially higher returns.  

**Specialised Mutual Funds:**  
- **Sector Funds:** Target specific industries or sectors believed to perform well.  
- **Index Funds:** Replicate the performance of a market index, providing low-cost exposure to the overall market or specific segments.  
- **Funds of Funds:** Invest in other mutual funds, offering diversified portfolios through a single investment.  
- **Emerging Market Funds:** Invest in developing economies, offering growth opportunities with higher risk.  
- **International or Foreign Funds:** Invest outside the investor’s home country, providing global diversification.  

KEY POINTS:  
- Mutual Funds are available in various structures: open-ended, closed-ended, and interval funds.  
- Risk levels range from very low to high, catering to different investor profiles.  
- Fixed Maturity Funds provide fixed returns with minimal interest rate risk.  
- Pension Funds help build retirement corpus through diversified investments.  
- Specialised funds allow targeted investment in sectors, indices, or international markets.  
- Open-ended funds offer liquidity; closed-ended funds offer potential tax benefits and fixed maturity.  
- Interval funds provide a hybrid option balancing liquidity and long-term investment.
