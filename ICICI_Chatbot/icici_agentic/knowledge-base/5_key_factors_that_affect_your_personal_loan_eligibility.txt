TITLE: 5 Key Factors That Affect Your Personal Loan Eligibility  
SOURCE: ICICI Bank  
CATEGORY: Personal Loans  
TAGS: Personal Loan, Eligibility, Income, Credit Score, Age, Work Experience, ICICI Bank  

CONTENT:  
Planning to take a Personal Loan? Understanding the eligibility criteria can improve your chances of getting assured personal loans with faster approvals. Personal Loans are useful for managing various expenses such as medical emergencies, weddings, vacations, education, or purchasing appliances and electronics. Unlike secured loans (Home Loan, Car Loan, Gold Loan), Personal Loans do not require collateral but depend heavily on your income and creditworthiness.

Key eligibility factors for Personal Loans at ICICI Bank include:

1. **Monthly Income**  
   - Adequate monthly income is essential to ensure timely repayment.  
   - Income requirements vary by location and are generally higher in metro cities like Mumbai, Delhi, and Bengaluru due to higher living costs.

2. **Credit Score**  
   - A credit score is a three-digit number reflecting your credit history and repayment behavior.  
   - ICICI Bank and most Indian lenders rely on the CIBIL score, which ranges from 300 to 900.  
   - A minimum CIBIL score of 700 is preferred for Personal Loan approval.  
   - Applicants with a score below 700 are advised to improve their credit score before applying.

3. **Age Criteria**  
   - Salaried applicants: 23 to 58 years.  
   - Self-employed professionals and non-professionals: 28 to 65 years (considering they can work beyond standard retirement age).

4. **Work Experience**  
   - More work experience indicates financial stability.  
   - Minimum experience requirements vary based on lender, occupation, and other factors.

KEY POINTS:  
- Personal Loans are unsecured loans requiring no collateral.  
- Minimum CIBIL score preferred: 700 and above.  
- Age eligibility: 23-58 years for salaried; 28-65 years for self-employed.  
- Income requirements vary by city, higher in metros.  
- Work experience is considered to assess financial stability.
