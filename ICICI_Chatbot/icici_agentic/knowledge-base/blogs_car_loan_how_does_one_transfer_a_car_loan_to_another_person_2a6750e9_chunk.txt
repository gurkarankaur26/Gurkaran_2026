CONTENT:  
Transferring a car loan to another person involves several steps and conditions. Both the current borrower and the new borrower must approve the transfer. The new borrower must meet the bank’s qualifying criteria and apply for a vehicle loan. Upon approval, the bank transfers the loan to the new borrower, who then assumes responsibility for future payments and the remaining loan balance. The car’s title must be updated to reflect the new ownership, and all related documentation should be completed by both parties.

When transferring a car loan, you may also need to transfer the insurance policy to the new borrower. This requires submitting bank documents to the insurance company to update the policy details.

Banks may charge processing fees and transfer fees for the loan transfer. These charges vary depending on the remaining loan tenure and principal amount. It is advisable to check with the lender for specific fees applicable to your case.

Car loan repayment tenure typically extends up to 7 years. If you find the loan repayment burdensome, you can opt for a Car Loan Balance Transfer facility. This allows you to refinance your vehicle loan with ICICI Bank at a lower interest rate, potentially reducing your monthly payments and overall interest costs.

FAQs:

1. Can the Car Loan balance be transferred?  
Yes, ICICI Bank allows the transfer of your car loan balance. This is usually done to obtain better loan terms or lower interest rates. The new lender pays off your existing loan and provides a new loan with updated terms.

2. Can I use a Balance Transfer to pay off a Car Loan?  
Yes, you can use a balance transfer to pay off your auto loan. This may help you secure better repayment terms or a lower interest rate, reducing your monthly payments and total interest expenses.

3. Is a Car Loan Balance Transfer a good idea?  
A balance transfer can be beneficial if it lowers your monthly payments, total interest costs, or offers better loan terms. However, consider any fees or costs associated with the transfer and evaluate your financial situation before proceeding.

KEY POINTS:  
- Loan transfer requires approval from both parties and bank’s consent.  
- New borrower must meet eligibility criteria and apply for a vehicle loan.  
- Car title and insurance policy must be updated post-transfer.  
- Processing and transfer fees may apply, varying by loan tenure and principal.  
- Car loan tenure is generally up to 7 years.  
- Balance transfer facility available to refinance at lower interest rates.  
- Balance transfer can reduce monthly payments and overall interest.  
- Evaluate costs and benefits before opting for a balance transfer.
