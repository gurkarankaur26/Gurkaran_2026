TITLE: Process for Rematerialisation of Shares and Related Services  
SOURCE: ICICI Bank  
CATEGORY: Demat Services / Share Rematerialisation  
TAGS: Rematerialisation, Physical Shares, Demat Account, Registrar, ISIN, RRF, Share Certificates  

CONTENT:  
ICICI Bank provides a detailed process for rematerialisation (remat) of shares, allowing customers to convert their dematerialised shares back into physical share certificates. The process involves submitting a Rematerialisation Request Form (RRF) at any ICICI Bank branch.

**Steps for Rematerialisation:**  
1. **Obtaining the RRF:**  
   - The Rematerialisation Request Form is available at all ICICI Bank branches.  
   - A separate RRF must be filled for each ISIN (International Securities Identification Number).  

2. **Filling the RRF:**  
   - Ensure the form is completely and correctly filled; incomplete forms may be rejected.  
   - Enter the correct name of the security and ISIN number.  
   - Submit separate RRFs for securities under lock-in and free securities. For lock-in securities with different release dates or reasons, separate RRFs are required.  
   - Clearly mention the lot type (Jumbo or Market Lot). If not specified, certificates will be sent in market lot.  
   - Ensure sufficient free balance in your demat account for the ISIN for which the RRF is submitted.  
   - The RRF must be signed by all account holders in the same order as the specimen signature with ICICI Bank.  

3. **Submitting the RRF:**  
   - Submit the RRF in triplicate at any ICICI Bank branch.  
   - An acknowledgement slip will be stamped and handed over after verification.  

4. **Receipt of Physical Shares:**  
   - The RRF is forwarded to the registrar for processing.  
   - Upon acceptance, the demat account will be debited, and physical share certificates will be sent directly to the customer by the registrar.  
   - Customers can track the status via ICICI Bank’s website (www.icicibank.com), IVR, phone, or branch visit.  
   - SMS alerts will be sent for success or failure of the request if mobile alerts are registered.  
   - Normal processing time is up to 30 days, but this may vary depending on the registrar.  
   - If physical shares are not received within 30 days, customers can contact ICICI Bank via phone or email for assistance.  
   - For delays beyond 40 days, ICICI Bank can provide the registrar’s contact details and the Remat Request Number (RRN).  

5. **Rejection of Remat Requests:**  
   - Requests may be rejected by ICICI Bank’s Central Processing Office or the registrar for various reasons.  
   - Customers will be informed of the reason for rejection.  
   - A fresh request can be submitted after resolving the issues causing rejection.  

6. **Additional Information:**  
   - ICICI Bank also handles repurchase of units of UTI US '64 bonds as per instructions from National Securities Depository Limited (NSDL).  
   - UTI has converted US64 units in demat form to 6.75% tax-free GOI US64 bonds. Requests related to rematerialisation, dematerialisation, or repurchase of these units are processed accordingly.  

KEY POINTS:  
- RRF must be filled separately for each ISIN and for lock-in and free securities.  
- Lot type (Jumbo or Market Lot) must be clearly mentioned.  
- RRF must be signed by all account holders matching specimen signatures.  
- Submit RRF in triplicate at any ICICI Bank branch.  
- Physical shares are usually received within 30 days; delays can be tracked and escalated.  
- Rejection reasons will be communicated; resubmission possible after correction.  
- SMS alerts available for request status if registered.  
- ICICI Bank facilitates repurchase of UTI US '64 units converted to GOI bonds.
