TITLE: Fixed Deposit (FD) - Nominee and Legal Heir Claim Process  
SOURCE: ICICI Bank  
CATEGORY: Deposits, Fixed Deposit, Nomination, Legal Heir Process  
TAGS: Fixed Deposit, Nominee, Legal Heir, Tax Deduction, Section 80C, Claim Process, KYC, Succession Certificate  

CONTENT:  
- The maximum amount eligible for tax deduction under Section 80C applies to Fixed Deposits.  
- In the event of the depositor’s demise, the Fixed Deposit continues to earn interest until settlement.  
- The nominee or legal heir can claim the deposit by submitting the necessary documents to ICICI Bank.  
- Required documents for legal heirs include:  
  - Original or notarized death certificate of the depositor  
  - Completed claim form  
  - KYC documents of the nominee or legal heir  
  - Succession certificate, probate, or will (if no nominee is available)  
- After verification of the submitted documents, the bank releases the funds as per regulatory guidelines.  
- There is no need to transfer an FD between branches since all accounts and deposits are maintained under a single Customer ID, making them accessible from any ICICI Bank branch or digital channel.  

KEY POINTS:  
- FD interest continues post depositor’s death until settlement.  
- Nominee/legal heir must submit death certificate, claim form, KYC, and succession documents if applicable.  
- Funds are released after document verification as per regulations.  
- Single Customer ID allows access to all deposits across branches and digital platforms.  
- FD investments qualify for tax deduction under Section 80C.
