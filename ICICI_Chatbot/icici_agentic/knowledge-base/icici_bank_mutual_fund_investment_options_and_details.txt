TITLE: ICICI Bank Mutual Fund Investment Options and Details  
SOURCE: ICICI Bank  
CATEGORY: Mutual Funds, Investment Products  
TAGS: Mutual Funds, Investment, SBI Funds, IDCW, Growth, Debt Funds, Short Term Funds, Ultra Short Duration, Overnight Funds  

CONTENT:  
This document provides detailed information on various mutual fund schemes available through ICICI Bank, primarily focusing on SBI mutual fund products. The schemes include different types of funds such as low duration, medium duration, ultra short duration, overnight, savings, and short term debt funds. Each scheme is available in multiple variants including Direct (Dir) and Regular (Ret) plans, with options for Growth and Income Distribution cum Capital Withdrawal (IDCW) frequencies such as daily (Dly), weekly (Wkly), fortnightly (Fortntly), and monthly (Mthly).

Key Mutual Fund Schemes and Variants:  
- SBI Magnum Low Duration Fund: Available in Weekly IDCW option.  
- SBI Magnum Medium Duration Fund: Available in Direct Growth and IDCW options.  
- SBI Magnum Ultra Short Duration Fund: Available in Direct Growth, Daily IDCW, Weekly IDCW options.  
- SBI Nifty 10 Year Benchmark G-Sec ETF: A government securities exchange-traded fund.  
- SBI Overnight Fund: Available in Direct Growth, Direct IDCW, Daily IDCW, and Regular IDCW options.  
- SBI Savings Fund: Available in Direct Growth, Direct IDCW, Weekly IDCW, and Daily IDCW options.  
- SBI Short Term Debt Fund: Available in Direct and Regular plans with Growth, Fortnightly IDCW, Monthly IDCW, and Weekly IDCW options.

Investment Features:  
- Multiple IDCW payout frequencies to suit investor preferences.  
- Availability of Direct plans which have lower expense ratios compared to Regular plans.  
- Growth options for capital appreciation.  
- Debt-oriented funds suitable for conservative investors seeking regular income or short-term investment options.  
- Government securities ETF for exposure to sovereign debt.

KEY POINTS:  
- Mutual fund schemes cover a wide range of durations and risk profiles.  
- IDCW (Income Distribution cum Capital Withdrawal) options available daily, weekly, fortnightly, and monthly.  
- Direct plans offer cost efficiency with lower fees.  
- Growth plans suitable for investors seeking capital appreciation.  
- Debt funds provide relatively stable returns with lower risk compared to equity funds.  
- SBI Nifty 10 Year Benchmark G-Sec ETF offers exposure to government securities with benchmark tracking.  
- Suitable for investors looking for systematic income or short-term investment solutions.
