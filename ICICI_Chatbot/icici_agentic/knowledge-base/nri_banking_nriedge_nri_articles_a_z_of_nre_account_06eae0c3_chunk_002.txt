ICICI Bank NRE Account - Features, Eligibility, Taxation, and Regulations

CONTENT:  
An NRE (Non-Resident External) account is a bank account in India that NRIs (Non-Resident Indians), PIOs (Persons of Indian Origin), and OCIs (Overseas Citizens of India) can open to park their foreign earnings in Indian rupees. The account allows free repatriation of funds and offers tax benefits under Indian laws.

**Who can apply for an NRE Account?**  
- Only NRIs, PIOs, and OCIs are eligible to open an NRE account.  
- Under FEMA regulations, an individual is considered an NRI if they meet the prescribed criteria for non-resident status.

**Account Features:**  
- Funds in the NRE account are freely repatriable without any limits.  
- Interest earned on the account is exempt from income tax in India.  
- The account can be used to deposit current income earned in India such as rent, dividends, pension, and interest, subject to applicable taxes.  
- Interest accrues on balances and investments made from the account in India.  
- Maturity or sale proceeds of permissible investments made from the NRE/FCNR (B) account or from remittances received from outside India through banking channels can be credited.  
- Funds can be used for outward remittances from India and disbursements within India such as managing family expenses.  
- Transfers are allowed to your own or other eligible NRE/FCNR (B) accounts.  
- Investments in India such as mutual funds, shares, securities, commercial papers of Indian companies, or immovable property are permitted in accordance with extant regulations.  
- Other transactions are allowed if covered under general or special permission granted by the Reserve Bank of India (RBI).

**Documentation Requirements:**  
- The list of documents required is indicative and may vary by bank.  
- Typically includes proof of NRI status, identity proof, address proof, and other KYC documents.  
- PAN card is mandatory if aggregate cash deposits/withdrawals in one or more accounts during a financial year exceed ₹2 million. If the applicant does not have a PAN card, they must initiate the application process.

**Taxation:**  
- Income earned in the NRE account is exempt from income tax in India.  
- Interest earned is tax-free.  
- PAN card is mandatory for high-value transactions as per Indian tax laws.

**Repatriation:**  
- Funds held in the NRE account are fully repatriable, meaning NRIs can transfer funds abroad without restrictions.

**Regulatory Compliance:**  
- As per FEMA regulations, NRIs who become Indian residents again must convert their NRE account to a resident bank account.  
- The account operates on a ‘former or survivor’ basis in joint accounts, where the former account holder primarily operates the account, and the survivor can operate it after the former’s demise.

**Definitions:**  
- Relative (as per Companies Act, 2013 Section 2(77)) includes parents (including stepparents), son (including stepson), son’s wife, daughter, daughter’s husband, siblings (including stepsiblings), and children.

**How to Open an NRE Account with ICICI Bank:**  
- Visit the nearest ICICI Bank branch or the bank’s official website to initiate the account opening process.  
- Follow the instructions and submit the required documentation.

KEY POINTS:  
- NRE accounts are exclusively for NRIs, PIOs, and OCIs.  
- Funds in NRE accounts are freely repatriable without limits.  
- Interest earned is exempt from Indian income tax.  
- PAN card is mandatory if cash transactions exceed ₹2 million in a financial year.  
- Investments and transactions are allowed as per RBI and FEMA regulations.  
- NRIs becoming Indian residents must convert the NRE account to a resident account.  
- Joint accounts operate on a ‘former or survivor’ basis.  
- Documentation requirements may vary; consult ICICI Bank for details.
