TITLE: ICICI Bank Reduces Home Loan Interest Rate to 6.70%
SOURCE: ICICI Bank
CATEGORY: Home Loans, Interest Rates, Banking Products
TAGS: Home Loan, Interest Rate, Digital Application, Mortgage, Instant Sanction, Video KYC, Loan Eligibility, Loan Amount, Customer Benefits

CONTENT:
ICICI Bank announced a reduction in its home loan interest rate to 6.70%, effective from March 5, 2021. This is the lowest interest rate offered by the bank in the last 10 years. The reduced rate applies to home loans up to Rs 75 lakh. For loans exceeding Rs 75 lakh, the interest rate starts at 6.75%. These rates were available until March 31, 2021.

Customers, including non-ICICI Bank customers, can apply for home loans digitally through the bank’s website or mobile banking platform ‘iMobile’. The bank offers a fully digitized home loan process, including instant sanction of loans, which can be accessed online or at the nearest ICICI Bank branch. The bank also introduced video KYC facilities during the pandemic to enable customers to onboard remotely without visiting a branch.

ICICI Bank has leveraged Big Data analytics to provide instant loans, including fresh loans, top-ups, and balance transfers, to millions of pre-approved customers. Approximately one-third of new home loans are sourced digitally. The bank’s mortgage loan portfolio crossed Rs 2 trillion in November 2020, marking a significant milestone. The bank’s mortgage disbursements reached an all-time monthly high in December 2020.

Home loan interest rates vary based on several factors such as credit bureau score, customer profile, and customer segment.

KEY POINTS:
- Home loan interest rate reduced to 6.70% for loans up to Rs 75 lakh.
- Interest rate for loans above Rs 75 lakh starts at 6.75%.
- Rates effective from March 5, 2021, to March 31, 2021.
- Digital application available via ICICI Bank website and ‘iMobile’ app.
- Instant loan sanction available digitally for all customers, including those from other banks.
- Video KYC facility introduced for remote onboarding.
- Mortgage loan portfolio crossed Rs 2 trillion in November 2020.
- One-third of new home loans sourced digitally.
- Interest rates vary based on credit score, customer profile, and segment.
- Bank offers fresh loans, top-ups, and balance transfers with instant approvals.
