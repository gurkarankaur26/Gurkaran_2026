CONTENT:  
A Home Loan is a long-term financial commitment typically repaid over 15 to 25 years. ICICI Bank offers Home Loans not only to salaried employees but also to self-employed professionals and business owners. Since self-employed applicants do not have a fixed monthly salary, their income verification involves additional criteria.

**Who is considered a Self-employed Home Loan applicant?**  
- **Professionals:** Chartered Accountants (CAs), Lawyers, Architects, Doctors, and other professionally qualified individuals running their own practice.  
- **Non-professionals:** Traders, Painters, Insurance Agents, Contractors, and others who run their own business but are not professionally qualified.

**Eligibility Criteria for Self-employed Home Loan Applicants:**  
- Minimum annual income: Rs. 3,60,000* (may vary by lender).  
- Fixed obligations (existing EMIs, rent, etc.) should not exceed 40-50%* of the monthly income to ensure affordability of Home Loan EMIs.  
- Higher income generally leads to higher eligible loan amounts, provided other eligibility conditions are met.

**Documents Required:**  
Self-employed applicants must submit income proofs such as:  
- Income Tax Returns (ITR) for the last 2-3 years  
- Profit & Loss statements and Balance Sheets (for business owners)  
- Bank statements  
- Professional receipts or business turnover documents  

**Home Loan Approval without Formal Income Proofs:**  
Applicants from informal sectors who lack formal income documents may still get loan approval if they:  
- Demonstrate gross professional receipts or business turnover  
- Have a strong credit score and good loan repayment history  
- Fulfill other eligibility criteria  

KEY POINTS:  
- Home Loan tenure: 15-25 years  
- Minimum income for self-employed: Rs. 3,60,000 per annum*  
- Fixed obligations should be ≤ 40-50% of monthly income  
- Both professionals and non-professionals can apply  
- Income proof documents required: ITR, financial statements, bank statements  
- Loan approval possible without formal income proof if other criteria are met  
- Good credit score and repayment history improve chances of approval
