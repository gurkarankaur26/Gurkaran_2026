CONTENT:  
ICICI Bank offers Non-Resident Indians (NRIs) the opportunity to invest in Indian mutual funds through its platform. Mutual fund investments are subject to market risks, and investors should carefully read all scheme-related documents before investing.

**Eligibility:**  
- Available to Non-Resident Indians (NRIs) who wish to invest in Indian mutual funds.  
- Investments are not offered to persons residing in the United States or Canada, or to US persons as defined under the US Securities Act, 1933, as amended.

**Investment Process:**  
- ICICI Bank acts as a distributor or corporate agent for third-party mutual fund products.  
- The contract for mutual fund investments is directly between the investor and the asset management company (AMC).  
- ICICI Bank facilitates the investment process but is not responsible for the performance or any loss arising from the mutual fund investments.  
- Customers must comply with the terms and conditions of ICICI Bank and the respective third-party asset management companies.

**Important Notes:**  
- Mutual fund investments carry market risks, including the potential loss of principal.  
- ICICI Bank does not provide legal, tax, securities, or investment advice.  
- The bank is not liable for any errors, losses, or damages resulting from reliance on the information provided or from the operations of the mutual funds.  
- Participation in mutual fund schemes is voluntary and independent of any banking services provided by ICICI Bank.  
- Investors should review all scheme documents and disclosures available on the ICICI Bank website before investing.

**Benefits:**  
- Access to a wide range of Indian mutual fund schemes through a trusted banking partner.  
- Convenience of investing in Indian mutual funds from abroad.  
- Support from ICICI Bank as an AMFI-registered mutual fund distributor.

KEY POINTS:  
- Mutual fund investments are subject to market risks; read scheme documents carefully.
