CONTENT:  
ICICI Bank offers Education Loans to cover various education-related expenses including tuition fees, books, travel, accommodation, hostel charges, and living expenses. The loan amount can be planned by separating mandatory academic costs such as tuition and examination fees from variable costs like travel and living expenses.

**Loan-to-Cost Ratio:**  
This ratio represents the share of your total education cost funded by the loan. To calculate, add all education expenses (tuition fees, hostel, travel, examination charges, study materials, and other related costs) and divide the Education Loan amount by this total cost. ICICI Bank offers zero margin for selected institutes, which may reduce or eliminate your upfront contribution for eligible expenses.

**EMI Calculator and Tools:**  
ICICI Bank provides an Education Loan EMI calculator that uses an RBI-standard formula to estimate monthly EMI, total interest payable, and total repayment amount. The calculator allows users to model different loan amounts, interest rates, and tenures to understand the impact on EMI and total repayment. Additional tools include:  
- Interest Calculator: Reviews total interest payable across the loan tenure.  
- Education Loan vs Self-Funding Comparison Tool: Helps compare funding options.  
- Tax Benefit Calculator: Estimates tax benefits on loan repayment.

The EMI calculator does not suggest an ideal loan amount based on projected post-graduation income but helps users test different scenarios to determine affordability based on their income expectations.

**Interest Rate Revisions:**  
If the interest rate is revised, ICICI Bank allows borrowers to:  
- Change EMI amount,  
- Change loan tenure, or  
- Change both EMI and tenure before the reset date.

This flexibility helps manage repayment according to changing financial circumstances.

**Loan Features and Considerations:**  
- Covers tuition fees, books, travel, accommodation, hostel charges, and living expenses.  
- Loan amount planning should consider mandatory and variable costs separately.  
- Borrowers should review loan amount, interest rate, repayment tenure, moratorium structure, covered expenses, and whether the loan is secured or collateral-free.  
- Eligibility calculators are available to estimate the loan amount one may qualify for.
