TITLE: ICICI Bank Mutual Fund Investment Options  
SOURCE: ICICI Bank  
CATEGORY: Mutual Funds  
TAGS: Mutual Funds, Investment, Debt Funds, Liquid Funds, Bond Funds, ICICI Bank  

CONTENT:  
ICICI Bank offers a variety of mutual fund investment options through partnerships with leading asset management companies. These funds include ultra-short duration funds, liquid funds, banking and PSU debt funds, bond funds, corporate bond funds, dynamic bond funds, floating rate funds, and gilt funds. Each fund has different plans such as Growth, Dividend (IDCW) with monthly, quarterly, weekly, or annual payout options.

Key mutual fund options include:  
- Invesco India Ultra Short Duration Fund (Growth, Monthly IDCW, Quarterly IDCW)  
- JM Liquid Fund (Bonus, Direct and Regular plans with Daily, Weekly, Quarterly IDCW, and Growth options)  
- Kotak Banking and PSU Debt Fund (Direct and Regular plans with Growth and Monthly IDCW)  
- Kotak Bond Fund (Direct and Regular plans with Growth and Annual IDCW)  
- Kotak Bond Short Term Fund (Direct and Regular plans with Growth and IDCW)  
- Kotak Corporate Bond Fund (Direct and Standard plans with Growth and Monthly IDCW)  
- Kotak Dynamic Bond Fund (Direct and Regular plans with Growth and Quarterly IDCW)  
- Kotak Floating Rate Fund (Direct and Regular plans with Growth and Monthly IDCW)  
- Kotak Gilt Fund (Direct and Regular plans with Quarterly IDCW)  

Investors can choose the plan and payout frequency that best suits their investment goals and income needs. These funds primarily invest in debt and money market instruments, offering relatively stable returns with varying degrees of risk and liquidity.

KEY POINTS:  
- Multiple mutual fund schemes available through ICICI Bank with various payout options (Growth, Monthly, Quarterly, Weekly, Annual IDCW).  
- Funds cover ultra-short duration, liquid, banking & PSU debt, bond, corporate bond, dynamic bond, floating rate, and gilt categories.  
- Plans available in Direct and Regular options.  
- Suitable for investors seeking income through dividends or capital appreciation through growth plans.  
- Investment in debt and money market instruments ensures relatively stable returns.
