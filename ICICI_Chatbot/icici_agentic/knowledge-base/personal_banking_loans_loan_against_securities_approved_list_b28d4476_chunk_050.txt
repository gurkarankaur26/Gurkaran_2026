UTI Mutual Funds Overview

TAGS:

CONTENT:  
This document provides a comprehensive list of UTI Mutual Fund schemes available for investment through ICICI Bank. The schemes include various types such as Growth, Dividend (IDCW), Direct Plans, and Sector-specific funds. These funds cater to different investor needs including equity, hybrid, and sectoral exposure.

Key UTI Mutual Fund Schemes:

1. **Hybrid Funds**  
   - UTI Banking and Financial Services Fund (Growth, IDCW, Direct IDCW)  
   - UTI Conservative Hybrid Fund (Growth, IDCW, Monthly IDCW, Flexi IDCW, Direct Plans)  
   - UTI Children’s Hybrid Fund (Growth, Direct Growth)

2. **Equity Funds**  
   - UTI Flexi Cap Fund (Growth, IDCW, Direct Growth, Direct IDCW)  
   - UTI Large Cap Fund (Growth, IDCW, Direct Growth, Direct IDCW)  
   - UTI Large & Mid Cap Fund (Growth, IDCW, Direct Growth, Direct IDCW)  
   - UTI Mid Cap Fund (Growth, IDCW, Direct Growth, Direct IDCW)  
   - UTI Focused Fund (Growth, IDCW, Direct Growth, Direct IDCW)  
   - UTI Dividend Yield Fund (Growth, IDCW, Direct Growth, Direct IDCW)

3. **Sectoral/Thematic Funds**  
   - UTI Healthcare Fund (Growth, IDCW, Direct Growth, Direct IDCW)  
   - UTI Infrastructure Fund (Growth, IDCW, Direct Growth, Direct IDCW)  
   - UTI BSE Sensex ETF

4. **Other Funds**  
   - UTI Childrens Hybrid Fund  
   - UTI Dividend Yield Fund

Each scheme is available in multiple plan options such as Regular and Direct, with dividend payout options including IDCW (Income Distribution cum Capital Withdrawal) and Growth.

KEY POINTS:  
- Multiple UTI Mutual Fund schemes are available for investment through ICICI Bank.  
- Schemes include Growth and Dividend (IDCW) options.  
- Direct Plans offer lower expense ratios compared to Regular Plans.  
- Hybrid funds combine equity and debt for balanced risk and return.  
- Sectoral funds focus on specific industries like Healthcare and Infrastructure.  
- Investors can choose monthly or flexible dividend payout options where available.  
- Suitable for investors with varying risk appetites and investment horizons.
