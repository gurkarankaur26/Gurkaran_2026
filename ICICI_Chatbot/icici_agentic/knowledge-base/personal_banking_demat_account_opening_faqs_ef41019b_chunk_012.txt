TITLE:  
Demat Account Nomination, Transmission of Shares, and NRI Guidelines

SOURCE: ICICI Bank  
CATEGORY: Demat Account Services / NRI Banking / Share Transmission  
TAGS: Demat Account, Nomination, NRI, Transmission of Shares, Inheritance, FEMA, NRO Account, PINS, RBI Approval  

CONTENT:  
1. **Change of Bank Details for Demat Account**  
- Customers must update their bank account details for debit of demat charges and credit of dividends, interest, IPO credits, etc.  
- To modify bank details, customers need to submit the "Change in Bank Details Form."  

2. **Nomination in Demat Accounts**  
- Nomination is mandatory for all High Net Worth Individual (HNI) customers.  
- Customers are strongly advised to have a nominee in their Demat accounts at the time of account opening.  
- The nominee or legal heir should have an active Demat account.  
- If the nominee is an NRI, they must open a Non-PINS Non-repatriable Demat Account.  

3. **Transmission of Shares (When Nomination is in Place)**  
- The nominee must submit a written request to the Depository Participant (DP) along with a certified true copy of the death certificate issued by a competent authority.  
- Shares will be transmitted to the nominee’s Demat account held with any depository.  
- For NRIs, shares transmitted to a Non-PINS Non-repatriable Demat Account can be sold in the secondary market without PINS or RBI permission.  
- PINS approval is only required for secondary market transactions, not for selling inherited shares.  

4. **NRI Guidelines on Holding and Transferring Shares**  
- As per FEMA guidelines, NRIs can hold, own, and transfer securities acquired while resident in India or inherited from a resident Indian without RBI permission.  
- Shares held on a non-repatriable basis will have sale proceeds credited to the NRO bank account.  
- NRIs can remit up to US $1 million per financial year from NRO accounts or sale proceeds of inherited assets, subject to documentary proof.  
- For remittances exceeding US $1 million, prior RBI approval is required along with supporting documents as per FEMA (Remittance of Assets) Regulation, 2000.  

KEY POINTS:  
- Mandatory nomination for HNI Demat accounts.  
- Change in bank details requires submission of a specific form.  
- Transmission of shares requires death certificate and written request.  
- NRIs must open Non-PINS Non-repatriable Demat accounts for nomination.  
- No RBI permission needed for NRIs to sell inherited shares in secondary market.  
- Sale proceeds credited to NRO account; remittance limits apply (US $1 million per financial year).  
- RBI approval required for remittance beyond prescribed limits.
