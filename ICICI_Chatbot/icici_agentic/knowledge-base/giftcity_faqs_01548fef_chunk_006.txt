CONTENT:  
ICICI Bank’s International Banking Unit (IBU) at GIFT City IFSC provides specialized banking and settlement services for NSE IFSC, India INX, and IIBX exchanges and their respective clearing corporations. The bank offers professional clearing services tailored for these exchanges.

Eligibility for availing IBU services includes:  
1. Corporate entities physically based in GIFT City IFSC and regulated by the International Financial Services Centres Authority (IFSCA).  
2. Entities incorporated outside India that are subsidiaries, holding companies, or joint ventures of India-based entities, where the India-based entities maintain linkages with ICICI Bank.  
3. Resident Indian entities permitted under the Foreign Exchange Management Act (FEMA) to hold foreign currency accounts outside India.

ICICI Bank IBU at GIFT City supports Indian importers and exporters by providing short to medium-term trade financing solutions. These solutions cater to both Open Account and Letter of Credit (LC)-backed trade requirements overseas.

The bank offers competitive pricing through one of India’s largest USD/INR interest rate (Non-Deliverable Forward - NDF) and options books. Additionally, ICICI Bank provides extensive market-making, trading, and hedging solutions to manage currency risk effectively.

For details on international investment options and remittance limits, customers are advised to consult their ICICI Bank Relationship Manager or refer to the Reserve Bank of India’s Liberalised Remittance Scheme (LRS) guidelines.

KEY POINTS:  
- Services available for NSE IFSC, India INX, and IIBX exchanges and clearing corporations.  
- Eligibility: Corporate entities in GIFT City IFSC regulated by IFSCA; foreign entities linked to Indian companies with ICICI Bank; Resident Indians permitted under FEMA.  
- Trade finance support for importers/exporters with Open Account and LC-backed options.  
- Competitive USD/INR interest rates and extensive currency risk management solutions.  
- Customers should consult Relationship Managers or RBI LRS guidelines for remittance and investment details.
