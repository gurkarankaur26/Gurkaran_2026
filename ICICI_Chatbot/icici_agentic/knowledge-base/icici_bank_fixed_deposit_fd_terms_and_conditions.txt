TITLE: ICICI Bank Fixed Deposit (FD) Terms and Conditions  
SOURCE: ICICI Bank  
CATEGORY: Fixed Deposit  
TAGS: Fixed Deposit, FD, Term Deposit, Interest Rates, Account Opening, Renewal, Maturity, ICICI Bank  

CONTENT:  
This document outlines the key terms and conditions governing Fixed Deposit (FD) accounts with ICICI Bank, applicable to both resident and non-resident depositors. It supplements specific terms provided during account opening and is subject to the Bank’s internal policies and RBI KYC guidelines.

**Account Opening:**  
- Subject to ICICI Bank’s internal policies and RBI KYC compliance.  
- Applicants must provide all required documentation for verification.  
- The Bank reserves the right to refuse account opening without providing reasons.

**Account Closure:**  
- Depositors can close the FD by submitting a written request. For joint accounts, all holders must sign.  
- The Bank may close accounts for unsatisfactory operation or other valid reasons after notifying the depositor.

**Term Deposit Receipt (TDR):**  
- A TDR or deposit advice is issued as proof of deposit.  
- It is non-transferable and not a negotiable instrument.  
- In case of loss or damage, the depositor must notify the Bank immediately and follow procedures for duplicate issuance, which may require indemnity.

**Interest:**  
- Interest rates depend on the deposit tenure at the time of booking.  
- Interest calculation method (simple or compounded) and payout frequency (monthly, quarterly, or at maturity) depend on the product chosen.  
- Interest rates are subject to change at the Bank’s discretion. Renewed deposits earn interest at rates prevailing on the renewal date.

**Renewal of Deposit:**  
- Depositors can opt for auto-renewal at account opening.  
- If auto-renewal is enabled, principal and/or interest is renewed for the original tenure at the prevailing interest rate on maturity.  
- If no instruction is given, the deposit is considered overdue after maturity.

**Repayment on Maturity:**  
- Unless auto-renewed, maturity proceeds are credited to the depositor’s designated Savings or Current account.  
- Payments are made according to operational instructions such as ‘Either or Survivor’ or ‘Former or Survivor’.

KEY POINTS:  
- FD accounts require KYC compliance and are subject to Bank’s internal policies.  
- TDR is proof of deposit but non-transferable.  
- Interest rates vary by tenure and product, and can change at Bank’s discretion.  
- Auto-renewal option available; otherwise, deposits become overdue on maturity.  
- Maturity proceeds credited to operative account unless auto-renewed.  
- Account closure requires written request; joint accounts need all holders’ consent.
