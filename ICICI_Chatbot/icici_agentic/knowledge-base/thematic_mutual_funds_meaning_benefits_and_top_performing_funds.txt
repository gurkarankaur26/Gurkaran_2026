TITLE: Thematic Mutual Funds: Meaning, Benefits, and Top Performing Funds  
SOURCE: ICICI Bank  
CATEGORY: Mutual Funds, Investment  
TAGS: Thematic Mutual Funds, Equity Funds, Investment Strategy, SEBI Guidelines, Risk, Diversification, Returns  

CONTENT:  
Thematic Mutual Funds are open-ended equity schemes that invest based on predetermined investment themes rather than traditional classifications like market capitalization or sector. These funds select stocks of companies aligned with a specific theme or idea, which may span multiple sectors. For example, a fund following an Environmental, Social, and Governance (ESG) theme invests in companies excelling in these factors across various industries such as technology, financial services, FMCG, or consumer durables.

According to SEBI guidelines, Thematic Funds must invest at least 80% of their total assets in equity and equity-related instruments related to the selected theme. These funds offer a balance between focused investment and diversification by including companies from multiple sectors that fit the central theme.

Thematic Mutual Funds are known for high growth potential when the chosen theme performs well, offering attractive returns during favorable market conditions. However, since they depend on a single theme, they carry higher risk compared to diversified equity funds and may underperform if the theme faces challenges.

Ideal investors for Thematic Funds are those with higher risk tolerance and good market awareness who can understand and track the fund’s performance. Thematic Funds provide more diversification than Sectoral Funds, which invest solely in one sector and are more vulnerable to sector-specific downturns.

KEY POINTS:  
- Thematic Mutual Funds invest based on a specific theme across multiple sectors.  
- Must invest a minimum of 80% of assets in equity/equity-related instruments related to the theme (SEBI guideline).  
- Offer potential for high returns if the theme performs well.  
- Higher risk compared to diversified equity funds due to theme concentration.  
- Suitable for investors with higher risk tolerance and market knowledge.  
- Provide more diversification than Sectoral Funds by spanning multiple sectors within the theme.
