Why is the Expected Rate of Return Important in a Mutual Fund Calculator?
The expected rate of return is a key factor in calculating future value.
Here’s why it matters:
Example – Impact of 1% difference over 10 years:
| SIP Amount | Tenure | Return Rate | Expected Value | 
| ₹10,000/month | 10 years | 10% | ₹20,14,576 | 
| ₹10,000/month | 10 years | 11% | ₹21,24,298 | 
You can start investing in mutual funds with as little as ₹500 per instalment through a Systematic Investment Plan (SIP).
This makes mutual fund investments accessible and flexible for all types of investors.
The calculator allows you to evaluate how returns differ between investing a fixed amount monthly (SIP) and investing a larger amount at once (Lumpsum).
Illustration:
Let’s assume an expected return rate of 12% over 5 years:
| Investment Type | Amount Invested | Duration | Expected Return | Maturity Amount | 
| SIP | ₹10,000/month | 5 years | 12% p.a. | ₹8.11 lakh | 
| Lumpsum | ₹6 lakh (one-time) | 5 years | 12% p.a. | ₹10.57 lakh | 
As shown, the investment method impacts your maturity value. The calculator helps you compare both options based on your investment capacity and goals.
