Our Home Loan EMI Calculator uses a simple mathematical formula to ensure accurate results for your financial planning.
Here’s the formula used:
EMI = [P x R x (1+R)^N] / [(1+R)^N - 1]
where:
P = Principal (Loan amount)
N = Loan tenure in months
R = Monthly interest rate
The monthly interest rate (R) is calculated as the annual rate of interest divided by 12 and then by 100.
Example: For a loan amount of ₹ 10,00,000, an illustrative annual interest rate of 7.25% and a tenure of 120 months, the EMI would be approximately ₹ 11,740.

Home Loan EMI amount is influenced by several factors, such as:
Interest Rate: A lower interest rate results in lower EMIs whereas a higher rate leads to higher EMIs.
Loan duration: A shorter tenure increases the EMI but decreases the total interest paid. A longer duration reduces your EMI but increases the overall interest paid.
Loan Amount: A higher Loan amount means a higher EMI; likewise, a lower amount means a lower EMI.
Credit Score: A higher credit score can help you secure a lower interest rate, which reduces the EMI amount.
Home Loan EMIs include repayment of the principal as well as the interest. Initially, EMIs focus more on interest, with later payments covering more of the principal amount.
The Home Loan EMI remains fixed for the entire loan tenure if you choose a fixed interest rate. However, in floating-rate loans, EMIs may change when the Home Loan interest rate fluctuates, depending on market conditions and the bank's policy.
