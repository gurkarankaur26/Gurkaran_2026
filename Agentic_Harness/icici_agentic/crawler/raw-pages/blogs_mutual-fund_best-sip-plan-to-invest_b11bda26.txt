SIP, or Systematic Investment Plan, is a popular way for beginners to start investing in mutual funds. It means investors can invest a fixed amount at regular intervals of time, generally monthly or quarterly. It is best suited for people who are looking to start their investment journey and trying to build wealth, planning for retirement, or have a set financial goal in mind.
SIP is a regular investing style in various investment instruments such as stocks and mutual funds. Most finance experts recommend it as one of the affordable and convenient ways to start investing in mutual funds. When you invest in small amounts every month, you have the opportunity to overcome any market volatility that might occur over time.
Before diving into SIP investment, several factors need to be taken into consideration:
Investment goals: Your financial goals will dictate the type of SIP suitable for you. For instance, Equity Funds might be better for the long-term growth, while Debt Funds could be suitable for short-term goals.
Risk tolerance: Your comfort with risk influences the fund choice. Higher risks (Equity Funds) may offer higher returns, whereas lower risks (Debt Funds) provide more stability.
Time horizon: The duration of your investment plays a critical role. Longer investment horizons can afford more risk, as there is time to recover from the market downturns.
Fund performance: Evaluating the past performance of a fund, although not a guarantee of future results, can provide insights into its management and strategy.
Here are a few key benefits of investing in SIPs:
SIPs are said to be a disciplined way of investing in mutual funds. Investing in SIPs every month gives you the freedom to take control of your finances and save a set amount of money monthly for investment.
The best part of SIP investment is that anyone can do it. Newcomers to the investment journey can start small, with amounts as low as Rs 500 per month. This eases the burden on your pocket while getting you ready for investments.
Another perk of SIP investment is that with low monthly amounts, you do not need to worry much about market fluctuations.
It is essential to understand that the monthly returns from SIP investments are reinvested in your investment amount until it matures. This compounding process helps grow your funds.

While SIPs are a smart and disciplined way to invest in mutual funds, it's equally important to understand their tax implications. Tax on SIP returns depends on the type of mutual fund (equity or debt) and the holding period of each investment.
In equity mutual funds, if units are held for more than 12 months, gains are treated as Long-Term Capital Gains (LTCG)—tax-free up to ₹1 lakh per financial year. Gains above ₹1 lakh are taxed at 10% without indexation. If units are sold before 12 months, returns are treated as Short-Term Capital Gains (STCG) and taxed at 15%.
For debt mutual funds, investments made on or after 1 April 2023 do not get long-term capital gains (LTCG) benefits — all gains are treated as short-term capital gains (STCG) and taxed as per your income tax slab, regardless of how long you hold them.
For investments made before 1 April 2023, units held for more than 36 months still qualify as LTCG, taxed at 20% with indexation. If held for 36 months or less, gains remain STCG and are taxed at slab rates.
Since each SIP instalment is considered a fresh investment, tax is calculated separately for each.
