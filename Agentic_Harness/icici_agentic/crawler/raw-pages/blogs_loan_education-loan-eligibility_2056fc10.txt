To qualify for an Education Loan from ICICI Bank, Indian nationals aged between 16 and 35 years, with a proven academic track record, having completed 10+2 years of education and pursuing undergraduate/postgraduate degrees or professional courses from recognised institutes in India or abroad, are eligible for an Education Loan. Co-borrowers such as parents, siblings, spouses or relatives can support the Loan application.
Even with 50% marks, individuals can avail an ICICI Bank Education Loan by meeting other eligibility requirements. Besides academic performance, factors such as confirmed admission status and a stable source of income from parents are important. Submitting documents such as admission letters and proof of income ensures a smoother application process. Additionally, involving co-borrowers strengthens the application. By fulfilling these criteria, applicants can enhance their chances of securing an Education Loan from ICICI Bank, despite academic setbacks.
While no minimum salary requirement exists for parents who are applying for an Education Loan for their children, they should be able to demonstrate a stable financial background to ensure repayment capability. ICICI Bank assesses the overall financial situation of applicants and co-borrowers to determine Loan eligibility.
Applicants can re-apply for an Education Loan if their initial application is rejected. To strengthen the case for approval, it's advisable to address any drawbacks in the application form and provide additional documentation or information, as requested by ICICI Bank.

The student’s course and institution directly affect education loan eligibility at ICICI Bank. Eligible courses include graduate, postgraduate, diploma, professional, technical, and job-oriented programmes, provided they are recognised by bodies such as UGC, AICTE, NAAC, NBA, Government authorities, AIBMS, or ICMR. The institution must also be recognised in India or abroad. Institute category and course type can influence the loan amount, interest rate, and whether collateral-free options may be available.
