Today, education is more than just a choice– it’s a key step in building a successful career. However, higher studies- especially overseas- can be costly. That’s where an Education Loan from ICICI Bank can help. It gives you the financial support you need to pursue your goals without any stress.
To get your loan approved smoothly, you will need to submit the documents required for an Education Loan. Missing or incorrect paperwork can result in delays or even your application being declined. That’s why it’s important to know which documents are required and keep them ready before you apply for the loan.
At ICICI Bank, the documents required for student loan can vary depending on the course, country and your financial situation. Here’s a simple guide to help you get started.
For a smooth and easy Education Loan application process, students and their guardians (in some cases) need to provide a set of documents. The following are the necessary documents required for an Education Loan from ICICI Bank:
Any one of Aadhaar Card, Voter ID, Driving Licence
Academic Documents: Marksheets, Degree Certificate(s), Entrance Exam Scores
Admission Proof: Offer / Acceptance Letter, Confirmation of Admission, I-20 (for USA) or similar admission confirmation documents.

Eligibility criteria for an Education Loan typically includes a person of Indian nationality, aged between 16 years and 65 years at the time of maturity (on case to case basis) having a good academic record with completion of 10+2 or an equivalent degree. The loan is applicable for pursuing graduation, postgraduate degrees or professional education diplomas from recognised and accredited institutes in India or overseas. Loan approval is based on pre-admission sanction, entrance exam scores and confirmed admission.
Co-borrowers can include family members like parents, siblings or spouses, while collateral may involve property, Fixed Deposits or existing ICICI Bank Home Loans. Specific documentation and proof of income might also be required.
Property documents are required only if you are opting for a secured Education Loan, where the property acts as collateral. A pledged property can help in availing a higher loan amount at a lower education loan interest rate.
Yes, an ITR (Income Tax Returns) document is required from self-employed individuals who are applicants / co-applicants / guarantors. It helps the Bank assess their financial stability and repayment capacity. An ITR document is also required for salaried individuals for Education Loans above a certain amount.
Proof of income includes salary slips and bank statements for salaried individuals. For self-employed individuals, the proof of income can be IT Returns, GST details and proof of business.
A gap certificate might be required if there is a break in your education history. It explains the reason for the gap and helps the Bank assess your academic continuity.
