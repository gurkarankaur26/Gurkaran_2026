A Credit Card is a payment instrument issued by the Bank that allows customers to make purchases on credit within a pre-set limit. Customers can repay either the total outstanding amount or the minimum amount due on or before the due date every month.
A Credit Card offers several benefits such as:
Convenient and secure payments
Interest free credit period (up to the billing cycle)
Rewards points and/or cashback
EMI payments
Credit score building
Exclusive shopping offers
Concierge services etc.
The credit period typically ranges between 18-48 days, depending on your billing cycle and the date of purchase.

A Contactless Credit Card enables tap-and-pay transactions at NFC-enabled POS terminals. During contactless payments, the card does not leave the customer’s hand, thus reducing fraud risk. Also, the low-value transactions can be performed without entering a PIN (as per regulatory limits).
A Secured Credit card is issued against a Fixed Deposit placed with the Bank. There should be no lien or partial withdrawal on the Fixed Deposit at the time of issuance. It is suitable for customers who have a limited credit history or want to build/repair their credit score. The secured card is issued only to primary account holders.
You can use your card to pay for your transactions at merchant- retail or online. Transactions are added to your account and reflected in the monthly statement. You then repay the dues by the due date.
The Credit Limit is the maximum amount you can spend on your card at any given time. It is assigned by the Bank based on eligibility criteria.
A billing cycle is the period between two statement dates and typically ranges from 28 to 31 days.
The Total Amount Due is the full outstanding amount on your Credit Card for the billing cycle.
It is the minimum sum you must pay by the due date to avoid late payment fees. Interest will continue to apply on the remaining balance.
The due date is the last date by which your payment must be made to avoid late fees and maintain a healthy account status.
It is the period from the transaction date to the statement due date when no interest is charged, provided the previous balance is cleared in full.

Revolving credit is a repayment option that allows you to pay only the minimum amount due and carry forward the remaining outstanding balance to the next billing cycle. Interest is charged on the unpaid portion until it is fully repaid.

Finance charges are interest costs applied to outstanding dues on your Credit Card. They may be levied on unpaid balances, cash advances, EMI conversions and amounts carried forward under revolving credit until fully repaid.
