. Environmental Science Overview
The Dynamic Nature of Ecosystems
Ecosystems are constantly evolving, reacting to both natural and human-induced changes. A healthy, balanced ecosystem can withstand minor disturbances, but extreme environmental shifts often cause lasting damage. Researchers monitor these changes to understand how to preserve biodiversity, focusing on indicator species that signal the overall health of a region.
Impact of Urbanization on Wildlife
As cities expand, natural habitats are fragmented, leading to a decline in local fauna. Urban planning that incorporates green spaces is vital for mitigating these effects. Efforts to build wildlife corridors allow species to migrate safely, maintaining genetic diversity even in developed areas.
